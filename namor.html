Namor
Complete Guide

The Atlantean King — who summons auto-tracking Monstro Spawn turrets, hurls tridents from mid-range, and calls Giganto to flatten entire teams with his ultimate.

If you want a Duelist who punishes dive characters automatically, melts tanks with relentless turret pressure, and can go completely invulnerable on demand — Namor rewards smart positioning and turret placement over raw mechanical skill.

Namor sits at A-Tier in the current meta — his Monstro Spawn turrets are uniquely powerful against dive-heavy compositions, and his sustained damage output consistently tops team scoreboards. His skill expression is almost entirely in turret placement: a Namor who hides turrets on walls at hard-to-reach angles is dramatically more effective than one who places them on the ground where enemies instantly destroy them. Kit Breakdown

Abilities & Skills

PASSIVE
Tide Fall
Namor falls more slowly when holding the jump button, giving him extended aerial hang time. This passive allows him to reposition vertically during fights, reach elevated turret placement spots, and avoid ground-level crowd control more easily than most Duelists.
💡 Use Tide Fall to reach elevated wall surfaces for turret placement — a Monstro Spawn stuck to a wall 4–5 meters up is significantly harder for enemies to destroy than one placed on the ground.
LMB
Trident of Neptune
Namor throws his trident forward in an arced trajectory, dealing damage on hit. The trident has no damage falloff — it deals full damage at any range — but the arc requires aim compensation at longer distances. Every hit on an enemy reduces the Aquatic Dominion cooldown, incentivizing constant primary fire. Headshots trigger Monstro Spawn berserk mode, increasing their attack speed temporarily.
Body damage: 75 Headshot: 140 Fire rate: 1.09/s Ammo: Infinite Falloff: None
💡 Fish for headshots aggressively — every headshot triggers berserk mode on your turrets without spending your secondary fire cooldown. Against Vanguards who are easy to hit, landing consistent headshots effectively doubles your turret DPS for free. [[1]](#__1)
RMB
Wrath of the Seven Seas
Namor launches a special trident that deals damage on impact and generates a spell field at the impact point. Most importantly, it directs all active Monstro Spawn to focus on the hit target and enter berserk mode — significantly increasing their attack speed for 2 seconds. This is Namor's single highest-damage ability when turrets are active, as the berserk state multiplies total DPS dramatically.
Projectile dmg: 40 Spell field dmg: 60 Turret focus: Yes Berserk: 2s Cooldown: 6s
💡 Never use Wrath of the Seven Seas without at least one turret active — the ability's damage multiplier is entirely dependent on turrets being alive. Save it for high-value targets: a Vanguard below 50% HP or a diving hero caught in your turret range. [[2]](#__2)
SHIFT
Blessing of the Deep
Namor summons a protective barrier while flying upward 7 meters, granting complete invulnerability for up to 3 seconds. During this window, he cannot be damaged, stunned, eaten, or affected by any enemy ability — including ultimates. He can still receive healing from allies while invulnerable. The cooldown is 20 seconds.
Duration: 3s Vertical: 7m Cooldown: 20s Invulnerable: 100%
💡 Save Blessing of the Deep exclusively as an escape tool — never use it proactively for repositioning. The moment you use it offensively, you have no escape for 20 seconds. The highest-value use is dodging enemy ultimates: it works against Jeff's swallow, Strange's stun, Punisher's ult, and every other ability in the game. [[1]](#__1)
E
Aquatic Domination
Namor summons a Monstro Spawn — a squid-like turret that autonomously attacks the nearest enemy within 40 meters. Each turret has 100 HP, lasts 8 seconds, and deals damage that falls off starting at 20 meters (reducing to 50% at 40m). Namor can have 2 turrets active simultaneously, with 2 charges that recharge individually. Every primary fire hit on an enemy reduces the charge cooldown.
Turret HP: 100 Duration: 8s Range: 40m Max active: 2 Charges: 2
💡 Always place turrets on walls rather than floors — wall-mounted turrets require enemies to look away from the fight to destroy them. Vary your placement every round so enemies don't develop a habit of instantly destroying them. Place one turret near your supports to automatically counter any dive attempt. [[2]](#__2)
Q — ULT
Horn of Proteus
Namor blows his horn to summon Giganto — a massive sea creature that leaps onto the targeted area after a 1.5-second delay. The inner circle (3.5m radius) deals 500 damage and disables all movement abilities for 1.5 seconds. The outer ring (9m radius) deals 180 damage and knocks enemies down. The 1.5-second delay gives enemies time to escape, making this ult highly telegraphed — but when combined with crowd control, the inner circle is an instant elimination for nearly every non-tank hero.
Inner dmg: 500 Outer dmg: 180 Hit delay: 1.5s Immobilize: 1.5s Range: 30m
💡 Never use Horn of Proteus without a teammate CC ability to lock enemies in place. Doctor Strange's Eye of Agamotto or Groot's root are ideal — stun the group first, then immediately activate Giganto. Without CC, enemies simply walk out of the inner circle during the 1.5-second delay. [[1]](#__1)
TEAM-UP
Gamma Charge — with Hulk & Iron Man
When Hulk charges Iron Man with gamma radiation and Iron Man activates Armor Overdrive, Namor receives a gamma upgrade that summons an extra Gamma Monstro — a supercharged turret that continuously fires gamma rays at locked-on enemies. The longer the rays hit a target, the higher the damage output, making it exceptionally powerful against tanks and grouped enemies.
💡 The Gamma Monstro's continuous ray damage scales up the longer it stays on target — prioritize using it against Vanguards who can't easily escape the lock-on. This team-up turns Namor's already high sustained damage into one of the strongest DPS windows in the game. [[2]](#__2)

Turret Setup Loop

  • 1
    Place first Monstro Spawn on a wall before engaging E pre-fight
    Always enter a fight with at least one turret already active. Use Tide Fall to reach elevated wall surfaces — a turret placed 4–5 meters up on a wall is dramatically harder to destroy than one on the ground.
  • 2
    Spam primary fire on the nearest Vanguard LMB ongoing
    Target tanks with your primary fire — large hitboxes make headshots easy to land, each hit reduces your turret cooldown, and headshots trigger berserk mode for free. Let your turret handle flankers automatically while you focus on the frontline.
  • 3
    Deploy second turret as soon as charge reloads E
    Place the second turret at a different angle from the first — two turrets covering different sightlines force enemies to choose which one to destroy, and whichever they ignore keeps firing freely.
  • 4
    Immediately redeploy destroyed turrets to new positions E
    When a turret is destroyed, never place the replacement in the same spot. Vary placement constantly — enemies who destroyed the first turret will look in the same place for the second one.
Pro tip: The turret loop is most effective when you treat turret placement as an active decision every 8 seconds, not a passive setup. The best Namor players are constantly repositioning turrets to angles the enemy team hasn't adapted to yet. [[1]](#__1)

Dive Counter Combo

  • 1
    Pre-place one turret near your supports E pre-fight
    Position one Monstro Spawn behind your team near your supports. When a dive hero like Spider-Man or Black Panther flanks, the turret starts firing automatically before you even see the threat.
  • 2
    Hit the diving enemy with Wrath of the Seven Seas RMB 0.0s
    The moment you see a dive hero enter your backline, immediately fire secondary fire at them. This directs all active turrets to focus on the diver and enter berserk mode — the combined auto-tracking damage is nearly impossible for most dive heroes to survive.
  • 3
    Follow up with primary fire headshots LMB ~0.3s
    While turrets focus the diver, add primary fire headshots to accelerate the kill. The diver is now taking turret auto-fire plus your trident damage simultaneously — most dive heroes cannot survive this combined burst.
  • 4
    Use Blessing of the Deep if the diver reaches you SHIFT
    If the dive hero closes the gap before dying, activate Blessing of the Deep immediately. The 3-second invulnerability window lets turrets finish them off while you take zero damage — then exit the ability and resume firing.
Why it works: Namor's auto-tracking turrets are the single best anti-dive tool in the Duelist roster. Unlike other heroes who need to manually aim at fast-moving targets, Monstro Spawn cannot miss — making Namor the natural counter to Spider-Man, Black Panther, and any other high-mobility flanker. [[2]](#__2)

Berserk Burst Rotation

  • 1
    Confirm both turrets are active before engaging a priority target pre-burst
    The berserk burst rotation only reaches its maximum damage potential with two turrets active. Never initiate this combo with zero or one turret — the damage difference is significant.
  • 2
    Land a headshot to trigger free berserk mode LMB 0.0s
    Aim for the head on your primary fire. A successful headshot triggers berserk mode on both turrets without spending your secondary fire cooldown — effectively giving you a free berserk activation every time you land a headshot.
  • 3
    Immediately follow with Wrath of the Seven Seas on the same target RMB ~0.2s
    Stack a secondary fire hit immediately after the headshot berserk trigger. This extends the berserk window and focuses both turrets on the target simultaneously — the combined DPS during this window is Namor's highest single-rotation output.
  • 4
    Continue primary fire until target is eliminated LMB
    Maintain primary fire throughout the berserk window. Each additional headshot extends the pressure and reduces turret cooldown simultaneously — keeping the rotation going continuously.
Why it works: The headshot berserk trigger is Namor's most efficient damage mechanic — it activates the same turret berserk state as secondary fire without spending the 6-second cooldown. Stacking headshot berserk into an immediate secondary fire creates a double-berserk window that no target at half HP can survive. [[1]](#__1)

CC-Chained Giganto Expert

Turning Horn of Proteus from an easily-avoided ult into a guaranteed team wipe through coordinated crowd control.

  • 1
    Coordinate with a CC teammate before activating (Groot / Strange)
    Communicate with your Groot or Doctor Strange before the fight. The plan is simple: they CC first, you ult immediately after. Without this coordination, Horn of Proteus is one of the most telegraphed and avoidable ultimates in the game.
  • 2
    Wait for CC to land on a grouped cluster 0.0s
    Only activate Horn of Proteus after you see the CC land and enemies are visibly locked in place. Activating before CC means enemies can still walk out during the 1.5-second delay.
  • 3
    Aim Giganto's inner circle on the center of the CC'd group Q ~0.5s after CC
    Place the inner circle (3.5m radius) directly on the grouped enemies. The 500 inner damage eliminates most non-tank heroes instantly. Even if some survive, the 1.5-second immobilize from Giganto sets up a follow-up burst from your team.
  • 4
    Immediately fire secondary fire on survivors RMB
    Any enemies who survived the outer ring are now immobilized and chipped. Hit them with Wrath of the Seven Seas to focus all turrets on the lowest HP survivor — the combined turret berserk plus primary fire finishes them before the immobilize expires.
Why it works: Horn of Proteus is uniquely powerful when paired with CC — the 1.5-second delay that makes it easily avoidable in solo play becomes irrelevant when enemies are rooted or stunned. A CC-chained Giganto on a grouped team is one of the highest single-ult team wipe potentials in Marvel Rivals, dealing 500 damage to every hero caught in the inner circle simultaneously.
