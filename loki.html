Loki
Complete Guide

The God of Mischief — a Strategist who heals through basic attacks, deploys autonomous healing clones, turns invisible at will, and copies enemy ultimates to turn their own power against them.

If you want a Strategist who can out-sustain entire dive compositions with clone uptime, vanish from any threat, and steal the enemy's ultimate to flip a round — Loki is the most deceptive support in the game.

Loki is rated S+ Tier at Diamond+ in Season 8 — the highest tier on the Mobalytics tier list — thanks to his unmatched healing ceiling and near-unkillable playstyle when played correctly. His skill expression lies entirely in clone placement, attack accuracy, and knowing exactly when to go invisible versus when to stay visible and pump healing. In the right hands, he produces more healing output than any other Strategist in the game.

Kit Breakdown

Abilities & Skills

PASSIVE
Glorious Illusion
Loki passively generates Illusion Charges over time, which are consumed to deploy Loki Clones. Each clone autonomously heals nearby allies at a fixed rate. Up to 2 clones can be active simultaneously. Clones persist until destroyed by enemy damage or manually recalled. The passive charge generation means Loki always has clone resources available as long as he manages them carefully rather than deploying recklessly.
💡 Think of your clones as a second and third healer on the field. Place them at the back of your team near the supports and tanks — not at the frontline where AoE will instantly delete them. A clone that survives for 15 seconds contributes far more healing than one that dies in 3.
LMB
Scepter of Mischief
Loki fires a projectile from his scepter that deals 55 damage to enemies on hit — but more importantly, heals allies on hit. This is his primary healing delivery mechanism. Unlike most Strategists who have passive or area healing, Loki must actively aim at teammates to heal them. Every successful hit on an ally restores a significant chunk of their health. Missing means zero healing delivered that tick.
Damage: 55 Heal on ally hit: ~65 HP Projectile: Yes Ammo: ∞
💡 Aim at your tank's body — it's the largest target and they need the most healing. Against dive threats, switch to healing the dived ally first, then return to the tank. The key habit is never letting your crosshair sit idle — always be firing at someone, enemy or ally.
RMB
Deception
Loki turns invisible and leaves behind a stationary illusion decoy at his current position. The invisibility lasts indefinitely — until Loki attacks, takes damage, or manually cancels it. The decoy left behind looks identical to Loki and draws enemy attention and fire. Enemies who shoot the decoy deal no meaningful damage while Loki repositions freely. This is the most powerful escape tool of any Strategist in the game.
Duration: Indefinite Breaks on: Attack / Damage taken Decoy: Yes — draws fire Cooldown: ~8s after break
💡 Never use Deception reactively when you're already at 50 HP — use it proactively the moment you see a diver commit. Activate it before they reach you, reposition to a new angle, and resume healing. The decoy buys you 2–3 seconds of free movement every time.
SHIFT
Loki's Tricks
Loki teleports to the location of one of his active clones, swapping positions with it. The clone remains at Loki's original position. This creates an instant repositioning tool that is completely unpredictable to enemies — they cannot track where Loki teleported because the clone stays visible at the original spot. Combined with Deception, Loki has two independent escape and repositioning tools, making him the hardest Strategist to pin down in the entire game.
Range: Unlimited (to clone position) Charges: 1 per active clone Cooldown: ~10s
💡 Always place at least one clone at a safe elevated position or behind cover specifically as a teleport destination — not just for healing. A clone on a rooftop or behind a wall gives you an instant escape route that no diver can follow without climbing themselves.
E
Regeneration Domain
Loki creates a healing zone at a target location that continuously heals all allies standing inside it. The zone lasts several seconds and stacks with clone healing — meaning allies inside the domain while clones are also active receive triple-layered healing. Pairing this ability with clone placement near a choke point or objective creates a near-unkillable sustain bubble for your team during pushes.
Duration: ~5s Heal: AoE continuous Cooldown: ~14s Stacks with: Clone healing
💡 Drop Regeneration Domain on the objective point during a contest, then teleport a clone next to it. Your team standing on the point now has two simultaneous healing sources — this is the highest sustained healing output Loki can produce and makes objective fights extremely difficult to win against him.
Q — ULT
God of Mischief
Loki transforms into a copy of a random enemy hero and gains access to all of that hero's abilities for a limited duration. This is the most situationally powerful ultimate in the game — if Loki copies Iron Man, he can fire Invincible Pulse Cannon. If he copies Doctor Strange, he can use Eye of Agamotto to stun the entire enemy team. The transformation is complete and functional, not cosmetic. The unpredictability of which hero gets copied is both the strength and the limitation of this ultimate.
Duration: ~12s transformation Copies: Random enemy hero Includes: All abilities + ult
💡 Activate God of Mischief when the enemy team has a high-value ult ready — Iron Man, Doctor Strange, Jeff the Land Shark. If you copy one of those heroes, you can fire their ultimate immediately. The best timing is right after the enemy uses a non-ult ability, when their ult meter is likely full from the fight.
TEAM-UP
Vibrant Vitality — with Thor
When paired with Thor, Loki gains access to Vibrant Vitality — a team-up enhancement that amplifies the healing output of both Loki and his clones when Thor is nearby. Thor's presence on the frontline drawing aggro while Loki's clones heal from behind creates one of the most self-sustaining frontline compositions in the game. Thor benefits from enhanced healing while Loki benefits from Thor absorbing all the dive pressure that would otherwise target him.
💡 In a Thor + Loki composition, position your clones directly behind Thor on the frontline. Thor draws all attention, clones heal Thor passively, and Loki stays at mid-range pumping additional healing through Scepter of Mischief. This three-layer healing loop is extremely difficult for enemy teams to break without a coordinated dive.
Matchup Analysis

Counters & Matchups

✅ Loki Excels Against
🕷
Spider-Man
Deception and clone teleport make Loki nearly impossible to pin down. Spider-Man's dive combo requires landing on a stationary target — Loki never is one.
🐾
Black Panther
Same reason as Spider-Man — dive heroes that rely on burst combos can't finish Loki before he vanishes or teleports to a clone.
💚
Hulk / Thor (enemy)
High-HP Vanguards can't burst down Loki's clones fast enough before the healing compounds. Sustained tank fights heavily favor Loki's HPS ceiling.
🎭
Ult-Dependent Teams
God of Mischief copies their best ultimate and fires it back. Teams built around one key ult (Iron Man, Strange) are particularly vulnerable.
❌ Hard Counters Loki
💥
AoE Burst Heroes
Clones have low HP — any AoE ability (Scarlet Witch ult, Iron Man ult) instantly destroys both clones, collapsing Loki's HPS from ~220 to ~70.
🎯
Psylocke
High single-target burst at close range can kill Loki before Deception activates. Her damage speed is faster than Loki's reaction window.
🔍
Reveal / True Sight Abilities
Any hero with a reveal ability (certain ults or passives) negates Deception entirely — Loki becomes a 275 HP target with no escape tool.
🏹
Long-Range Snipers
Loki's healing requires staying within mid-range. Snipers force him back, reducing his effective healing angle and clone placement options.

Advanced Techniques

Combo Guide

Clone + Scepter Loop

  • 1
    Deploy both clones at the start of every fight pre-fight
    Before any engagement begins, place both clones behind your frontline tanks. This establishes your passive healing foundation before you need to aim at anything.
  • 2
    Fire Scepter of Mischief at your tank continuously LMB ongoing
    Your tank is the primary healing target. Keep your crosshair on their body and fire constantly. Clones heal passively — your job is to stack active healing on top of them.
  • 3
    Switch to a dived ally immediately when a diver commits LMB
    The moment a Black Panther or Spider-Man dives your backline, redirect all scepter fire to the dived ally. Clones will continue healing the tank passively — your active healing is the emergency resource.
  • 4
    Redeploy destroyed clones immediately on cooldown
    The moment a clone dies, redeploy it as soon as the charge is available. Every second without 2 active clones is a significant HPS loss. Clone uptime is the single most important mechanical habit for Loki.
Pro tip: By the numbers, Loki should have the highest healing output in the game with max clone uptime — approximately 220 HPS. The reason many Loki players underperform is poor clone placement and letting clones die without immediately redeploying them.

Regen Domain + Clone Stack

  • 1
    Identify the objective contest point pre-push
    This technique is designed for objective fights — payload stops, capture points. Identify where your team will be standing for the next 5–8 seconds.
  • 2
    Place both clones on the objective point Passive charges
    Deploy clones directly on or adjacent to the objective. They will passively heal every ally contesting the point without Loki needing to aim at them.
  • 3
    Drop Regeneration Domain on the objective E 0.0s
    Activate the healing zone directly on top of your clones. Allies on the point now receive clone healing + domain healing simultaneously — the highest burst healing output Loki can produce.
  • 4
    Continue firing Scepter at your tank LMB
    While the domain and clones handle the objective, keep your active scepter healing on your tank who is likely taking the most damage. Three simultaneous healing sources make this objective fight nearly impossible to win against.
Why it works: Stacking clone healing + Regeneration Domain creates a healing density that exceeds what most enemy teams can burst through. The objective becomes a near-unkillable zone for your team as long as clones survive.

Deception Bait + Teleport Escape

  • 1
    Pre-place one clone at an elevated escape position
    Before the fight, place one clone on a rooftop, ledge, or behind cover specifically as a teleport destination — not for healing. This is your emergency exit.
  • 2
    Activate Deception the moment a diver commits RMB 0.0s
    Go invisible before the diver reaches you — not after. The decoy stays at your original position. The diver will attack the decoy for 2–3 seconds while you move freely.
  • 3
    Teleport to the escape clone SHIFT
    While invisible, teleport to your pre-placed escape clone. You are now on a rooftop or behind cover — completely out of the diver's reach — while the decoy still draws their attention below.
  • 4
    Resume healing from the new position
    The diver is now confused, attacking a decoy, while you're already back to pumping healing from a safe elevated angle. Redeploy a new clone at the escape position to restore your escape route for the next dive.
Why it works: Most dive heroes have a single burst window — if they can't kill you in that window, they've wasted their cooldowns and are now vulnerable. Deception + teleport guarantees you survive every dive attempt as long as you activate Deception before taking damage, not after.

God of Mischief Ult Copy Timing Expert

The highest skill expression on Loki — reading enemy ult timings to copy the most valuable ultimate at the right moment.

  • 1
    Track enemy ult charge throughout the fight
    Watch for visual and audio cues that high-value enemies (Iron Man, Doctor Strange, Jeff) are approaching full ult charge. The more damage they deal, the faster they charge.
  • 2
    Hold God of Mischief until the enemy ult is ready Q
    Do not activate God of Mischief early. The random copy is most valuable when the enemy hero you copy has their own ult ready to fire. Patience is the entire skill expression here.
  • 3
    Activate and immediately fire the copied ultimate 0.0s
    The moment you transform, check if the copied hero's ult is available. If it is — fire it immediately. Don't waste time using basic abilities in the transformed state.
  • 4
    Return to Loki and resume healing
    After the copied ult fires, the transformation ends. Immediately return to clone management and scepter healing — the team still needs you as a support after the ult play.
Why it works: God of Mischief is the only ultimate in the game that can fire two Iron Man ults, two Doctor Strange ults, or two Jeff cluster ults in a single round. In coordinated play, this creates a double-ult combo that no team can survive. The randomness is the only limiting factor — which is why timing the activation when the highest-value enemy has their ult ready is the entire skill expression.
Common Questions

FAQ

Loki is a medium-difficulty Strategist. His clone passive makes him forgiving — even if you miss scepter shots, clones provide baseline healing. The challenge is clone management: knowing where to place them, keeping them alive, and redeploying them instantly when they die. If you're new to support, start with a simpler healer like Mantis, then transition to Loki once you understand positioning fundamentals.

The most common reason is clone placement — if clones are placed too far from your team, or in positions where AoE damage instantly destroys them, your HPS collapses from ~220 to ~70. Check two things: (1) are both clones always active, and (2) are they within healing range of your frontline? A clone that survives for 15 seconds contributes far more than one that dies in 3 seconds from enemy splash damage.

Use Deception proactively — the moment you see a diver commit, not after they've already hit you. Activating it at 50 HP is too late; activating it when a Spider-Man starts their swing toward you is correct. The decoy buys you 2–3 seconds of free movement every time. Also use it to reposition between fights without being tracked — going invisible mid-rotation makes you nearly impossible to assassinate.

The best setup is waiting until a high-value enemy — Iron Man, Doctor Strange, or Jeff the Land Shark — has their ultimate fully charged. Activate God of Mischief, and if you copy one of those heroes, immediately fire their ultimate. In coordinated play, your team can chain their own ultimates right after yours for a double-ult combo that no team survives. Never waste God of Mischief early in a fight when enemy ult meters are empty.

Clone survival is entirely about placement. Never put clones at the frontline where AoE abilities will instantly delete them. Place them behind your tanks, on elevated positions, or tucked into corners where only targeted fire can reach them. A clone behind a Groot wall or on a rooftop survives dramatically longer than one standing in the open. Also use Loki's Tricks to teleport away from a clone that's about to be destroyed — saving the teleport charge for a new escape route.
