Rocket Raccoon
Complete Guide

The tech genius of the Guardians — the easiest Strategist to pick up in Marvel Rivals, with bouncing healing orbs, a team-wide revival beacon, and a B.R.U.T.E. mech ultimate that turns him into a frontline powerhouse.

If you want a Strategist who can heal the entire team with a single bounced orb, revive fallen allies mid-fight with a beacon, and then hop into a giant mech to carry the final push — Rocket Raccoon is the most beginner-friendly support with a surprisingly high skill ceiling.

Rocket Raccoon is rated A-Tier and is consistently ranked as the easiest Strategist in Marvel Rivals — his bouncing healing orbs require minimal aim precision, his beacon provides passive team buffs automatically, and his kit has more utility packed into it than any other support. The skill ceiling lies in beacon placement timing, orb bounce geometry, and knowing when to enter the B.R.U.T.E. mech versus staying in support mode.

Kit Breakdown

Abilities & Skills

PASSIVE
Furry Fury
Rocket's small size and agile movement make him naturally harder to hit than any other Strategist. His hitbox is significantly smaller than heroes like Loki or Mantis, meaning aimed shots that would connect on other supports frequently miss Rocket entirely. This passive survivability advantage is most pronounced against snipers and heroes who rely on precise aim — they must actively adjust their crosshair placement downward to track him effectively.
💡 Exploit your small hitbox by staying in constant motion — strafe side to side while healing. Against snipers specifically, crouch-walking while firing orbs makes you one of the hardest targets in the game to hit at long range. Most players aim at chest height; Rocket's head is at knee height for most heroes.
LMB
Healing Blaster
Rocket fires healing orbs that bounce off walls and terrain up to 3 times before expiring. Each orb heals the first ally it contacts, then continues bouncing to heal additional allies on subsequent bounces. In enclosed spaces with multiple walls, a single orb can heal 3 different allies in rapid succession. The orbs also deal modest damage to enemies on contact, making them dual-purpose in close-range fights. The bouncing mechanic is what makes Rocket the most forgiving healer — missed direct shots still heal allies around corners.
Heal per orb: ~55 HP Bounces: 3 max Enemy damage: ~35 Ammo: ∞ Multi-target: Yes
💡 In enclosed spaces and corridors, aim orbs at the wall behind your allies rather than directly at them — the bounce angle often reaches allies around corners that a direct shot couldn't. On open maps, aim at the floor near clustered allies for a guaranteed ground-bounce that catches multiple targets.
RMB
Jetpack Dash
Rocket activates his jetpack for a rapid burst dash in any direction, covering significant distance in a fraction of a second. This is his primary escape tool against divers. Unlike most support mobility abilities, the dash has a very short cooldown — allowing multiple uses in quick succession to completely reposition around a fight. The dash also has a slight upward component, allowing Rocket to reach elevated positions that he couldn't access on foot.
Distance: ~8m burst Direction: Any Cooldown: ~6s Invulnerable: No
💡 Use Jetpack Dash to reach Groot's shoulder during Flora Colossus — the elevated platform plus Groot's massive HP pool makes this the safest healing position in the game. Rocket firing healing orbs from Groot's shoulder during the ult window is nearly impossible for enemies to interrupt without first destroying the enormous Colossus.
SHIFT
Energized Beacon
Rocket deploys a small tech beacon at his current location that provides passive buffs to all nearby allies: increased movement speed and enhanced jump height. The beacon persists until destroyed by enemy fire or manually recalled. Allies within range of the beacon move faster and jump higher — the jump height bonus is particularly valuable for reaching elevated positions and dodging ground-level AoE attacks. Deploying the beacon at the start of every round before the fight begins is one of the highest-value habits in Rocket's kit.
Speed boost: ~15% Jump boost: Significant Range: ~12m radius Duration: Until destroyed Cooldown: ~12s after destruction
💡 Deploy the Energized Beacon at the very start of every round before any fighting begins — the movement speed and jump height buffs apply to your entire team's approach. A team with beacon buffs reaches the objective faster, jumps over cover more easily, and dodges AoE attacks that a non-buffed team would take. This single habit is worth more than most mechanical improvements.
E
Repair Mode
Rocket tosses a revive pack to a fallen ally, bringing them back to life at reduced HP at their death location. This is the only non-ultimate revive ability in the game — every other hero who can revive requires their ultimate to do so. The revive has a moderate cooldown and requires line of sight to the fallen ally's position. The reduced HP on revival means the revived ally needs immediate healing support, but being alive at 30% HP is infinitely better than being dead waiting for respawn.
Revive HP: ~30% max HP Cooldown: ~20s Requires: Line of sight Type: Non-ultimate revive
💡 Prioritize reviving your tank over your DPS — a revived tank at 30% HP with your healing orbs can survive long enough to matter, while a revived DPS at 30% HP often dies immediately to the next burst. Also, never revive into an active teamfight where the revived ally will instantly die again — wait for a brief lull in damage before throwing the revive pack.
Q — ULT
B.R.U.T.E.
Rocket calls in a B.R.U.T.E. mech suit — either piloting it himself or offering it to a teammate. The mech dramatically increases size, HP, and damage output, transforming whoever pilots it into a frontline powerhouse for the duration. When Rocket pilots it himself, he gains a massive gun with high DPS and significant AoE. When offered to a teammate, it amplifies their existing kit with the mech's stats. The mech is a large target that experienced enemies focus immediately, so positioning during the ult window is critical.
Duration: ~10s Pilot: Rocket or teammate HP increase: Significant DPS: High burst
💡 Offer the B.R.U.T.E. mech to your tank rather than piloting it yourself — a Groot or Thor in the mech becomes nearly unkillable while Rocket continues healing from behind. If no teammate takes it within 2 seconds, pilot it yourself. Never offer it to a support — they lack the positioning instincts to survive in a large, high-priority target.
TEAM-UP
Rocket Network 2099 — with Star-Lord & Groot
When paired with Star-Lord, Rocket's tech upgrades Star-Lord's Element Guns — increasing their damage and adding effects to Explosive Shot. When paired with Groot, Rocket can ride on Groot's shoulder as a mobile elevated healing platform. Both team-ups directly address the core weaknesses of their paired heroes: Star-Lord's reload penalty and Groot's inability to heal himself. These are two of the strongest team-up pairings in the game for their respective heroes.
💡 In a triple-Guardian composition (Groot + Star-Lord + Rocket), all three team-up bonuses activate simultaneously. Rocket rides Groot's shoulder for safety, Star-Lord gets upgraded guns, and Groot gets passive repair support. This is one of the most synergistic three-hero combinations in Marvel Rivals — each hero directly solves another's weakness.

AoE abilities don't care about Rocket's small hitbox — splash damage hits him regardless. Iron Man's ult, Scarlet Witch's Reality Erasure, and Jeff's cluster ult all kill him instantly.

Any hero who identifies and destroys the Energized Beacon immediately removes Rocket's passive team buff. A destroyed beacon with a 12-second cooldown means 12 seconds of no speed or jump bonus for the entire team.
🐾
Black Panther
Dive speed exceeds Jetpack Dash reaction time. His burst combo at close range can eliminate Rocket before the dash fully activates — the only consistent assassin who beats Rocket's mobility.

Beacon + Orb Loop

  • 1
    Deploy Energized Beacon at round start SHIFT pre-fight
    Before any engagement, drop the beacon at the team's staging area. Every ally in range immediately gains movement speed and jump height — the highest-value single action Rocket can take before a fight begins.
  • 2
    Fire healing orbs at your tank continuously LMB ongoing
    Your tank is the primary healing target. Aim orbs slightly to the side of them so bounces reach nearby allies simultaneously. Never let the tank drop below 50% HP before redirecting all orbs to them.
  • 3
    Redeploy beacon immediately if destroyed SHIFT
    The moment an enemy destroys your beacon, redeploy it on cooldown. Every second without the beacon is a second your team moves slower and jumps lower. Beacon uptime is the single most important habit for Rocket.
  • 4
    Jetpack Dash away from any diver immediately RMB
    The moment a diver commits, dash away before they reach you. Don't wait until you're taking damage — activate the dash the moment you see the dive animation begin. Resume orb healing from the new position.
Pro tip: The beacon + orb loop is deceptively simple but produces A-Tier results when executed consistently. Most Rocket players underperform because they forget to redeploy the beacon after it's destroyed. Beacon uptime is worth more than perfect orb aim.

Bounce Geometry Healing

  • 1
    Identify walls and geometry near your allies pre-shot
    Before firing, look for walls, floors, and structures near your allies. An orb bounced off the wall behind a grouped team heals more targets per shot than a direct hit on a single ally.
  • 2
    Aim at the wall behind your allies rather than directly at them LMB
    Fire at the wall surface behind your team. The orb bounces forward and heals the first ally it contacts, then continues bouncing to reach additional allies. In enclosed spaces, one orb can heal 3 different allies in rapid succession.
  • 3
    Use floor bounces for grouped allies on objectives LMB
    On open objective points with no walls, aim at the floor directly under a cluster of allies. The ground bounce catches multiple targets simultaneously — more reliable than direct shots when allies are tightly grouped.
  • 4
    Heal around corners with angled wall bounces
    Allies hiding behind cover can be healed without line of sight — fire an orb at the wall adjacent to the corner and angle it to bounce around. This is Rocket's most unique mechanical advantage over every other Strategist.
Why it works: Rocket's bouncing orbs are the only healing ability in the game that can heal allies around corners without line of sight. In maps with tight corridors and enclosed spaces, a single well-angled orb heals more total HP per second than any other Strategist's primary fire.

Revive Timing + Orb Cover

  • 1
    Identify the fallen ally's position and threat level 0.0s
    Before throwing Repair Mode, assess whether the revive location is safe. If 3 enemies are standing on the fallen ally's body, the revived ally will die instantly — wasting the 20-second cooldown. Wait for a lull in damage first.
  • 2
    Throw Repair Mode pack to the fallen ally E
    Prioritize reviving your tank over DPS. A revived tank at 30% HP with orb support can survive and matter. Aim the pack precisely — it requires line of sight and accurate targeting to connect.
  • 3
    Immediately redirect all orbs to the revived ally LMB ~0.5s
    The revived ally has ~30% HP and is the highest-priority healing target the moment they stand up. Redirect every orb to them until they reach at least 60% HP — then return to your normal healing rotation.
  • 4
    Communicate the revive to your team immediately
    Ping or call out the revive so teammates know the revived ally needs cover for 3–4 seconds while you heal them to a survivable HP level. A revived tank who gets immediately focused dies before the revive has any value.
Why it works: Repair Mode is the only non-ultimate revive in the game. Used correctly, it turns a 5v4 disadvantage back into a 5v5 mid-fight — denying the enemy team the kill they committed resources to secure. The 20-second cooldown means every use must be deliberate: never revive into active burst damage.

B.R.U.T.E. Mech Handoff Expert

Maximizing the B.R.U.T.E. mech by offering it to the right teammate at the right moment — the highest-impact play Rocket can make.

  • 1
    Identify the best mech recipient before activating
    The ideal recipient is your tank — Groot or Thor. They have the positioning instincts to survive in a large high-priority target, and the mech's HP increase stacks on top of their already-high base HP for a near-unkillable frontline.
  • 2
    Activate B.R.U.T.E. and offer to your tank immediately Q
    Activate the ult and immediately look at your tank to offer them the mech. They have 2 seconds to accept. If they accept, you continue healing from behind while they pilot the mech — the ideal outcome.
  • 3
    If no one accepts within 2 seconds, pilot it yourself ~2s
    Don't wait longer than 2 seconds — enter the mech yourself and push the objective immediately. Position behind your tank so you're not the primary focus target. Fire the mech's gun at grouped enemies while your support partner covers healing.
  • 4
    Exit the mech before it's destroyed to preserve Rocket's HP
    When the mech is at ~20% HP, exit it manually rather than letting it be destroyed. Exiting preserves Rocket's full base HP — letting the mech explode while you're inside deals significant damage to Rocket himself.
Why it works: A tank piloting the B.R.U.T.E. mech while Rocket heals from behind is the highest-durability frontline combination in the game. The tank's positioning instincts keep the mech alive longer, Rocket's orbs heal the mech's pilot, and the enemy team must focus an enormous high-HP target while Rocket continues supporting from a safe distance.
