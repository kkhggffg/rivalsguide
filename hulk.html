Hulk
Complete Guide

Bruce Banner — the most mobile and most challenging Vanguard in Marvel Rivals. Hulk isn't just a frontline tank; he's a jack-of-all-trades who can dive, brawl, or support depending on what the fight demands.

If you want the highest skill ceiling of any tank — a hero who rewards perfect timing, precise jump tech, and game sense with 1500 HP ult-form dominance — Hulk is the most rewarding Vanguard to master.

Hulk has 800 base HP — the highest of any Vanguard — and jumps to 1500 HP in HULK SMASH ult form. His Incredible Leap is the defining ability of his kit: mastering the jump charge tech makes him the most mobile tank in the game, capable of diving backlines and escaping before the enemy team can respond. The catch: he has a two-form mechanic — getting burst down forces him into fragile Bruce Banner form, which is the lowest HP state in the game. [[3]](#__3)

Kit Breakdown

Abilities & Skills

LMB
Heavy Blow (Punch)
DMG: 40/hit
Range: Melee (large radius)
Kills support at: ~7 hits
Hulk's basic melee punch dealing 40 damage per hit with a very large hit radius. It takes approximately 7 hits to kill a 250 HP support — low individual damage, but the wide radius means you don't need precise aim. Best used for sustained pressure on targets you've already cornered. [[3]](#__3)
💡 Don't rely on Heavy Blow alone for kills — it's too slow. Use it to pressure targets while your Gamma Burst and Radioactive Lockdown set up the actual kill. The wide radius is your friend against fast-moving supports.
SHIFT
Incredible Leap
Type: Charged Jump
Tech: Tap to pre-charge
Role: Primary Mobility
Hulk charges up and launches himself in any direction. This is the most important ability in his kit — mastering it makes him the most mobile tank in the game. The key tech: go into settings and change the jump to "tap to charge" mode. This lets you have the jump pre-charged at all times, ready to fire instantly when you need to escape or engage. [[3]](#__3)
💡 Change your jump settings to "tap to charge" immediately — this is non-negotiable for high-level Hulk play. With it, you can escape the enemy backline in under 0.5 seconds. Without it, you'll be too slow to get out before being focused down. [[3]](#__3)
RMB
Indestructible Guard (Gamma Shield)
Shield HP: 200 (self)
Ally Shield: 100 HP
Duration: 2s
Ult Charge: 100% of damage absorbed
Hulk raises a Gamma Shield that absorbs 200 damage for himself and grants 100 HP to nearby allies for 2 seconds. The critical mechanic: 100% of damage absorbed by the shield converts directly to ult charge — making it the fastest ult-farming tool in the game. Also blocks burst ultimates entirely: Jeff's ult, Iron Man's ult, Thing's ult, and others. Does NOT stop sustained DPS ults like Punisher or Storm. [[3]](#__3)
💡 Shield INTO burst ultimates — don't panic and run. If you see Jeff winding up, raise your shield and absorb the entire ult while charging your own. This is the single highest-value play Hulk can make in a teamfight. [[3]](#__3)
E
Radioactive Lockdown (Stun)
DMG: 5 (negligible)
Effect: Stun / Exile
Warning: Stunned target immune to ally DMG
Hulk stuns/exiles a target for a short duration. Critical caveat: while stunned, the target is immune to ALL damage from your allies — only Hulk himself can damage them during the exile. This is a double-edged sword: misuse wastes your team's burst window. Best used to escape sticky situations, save yourself from being chased, or stop key ultimates (Wanda, Scarlet Witch, and with timing — Iron Man). [[3]](#__3)
💡 Never stun a target your team is actively bursting — you'll make them invincible and waste your team's damage. Use Radioactive Lockdown to escape (stun the enemy chasing you), to stop ults, or to lock down a fleeing low-HP support so your Gamma Burst can finish them. [[3]](#__3)
F
Gamma Burst (Clap)
DMG: 70
Cooldown: 8s
Pierces: Multiple targets
Blocked by: Mag/Strange/Cap shields
Hulk claps his hands together and fires a semi-fast piercing projectile dealing 70 damage that passes through multiple enemies. Excellent for pressuring the enemy backline through their frontline tanks — the pierce means their tanks can't simply body-block it. Best used as a finisher on fleeing low-HP targets or to apply pressure on grouped enemies. [[3]](#__3)
💡 Combo: Radioactive Lockdown on a fleeing low-HP support → immediately Gamma Burst for the kill. The stun prevents them from dodging and the 70 damage finishes most supports. Save this for finishes — don't waste it as an opener. [[3]](#__3)
BANNER
Bruce Banner Form
HP: Lowest in game
Tools: Gamma Grenade + Gun
Self-grenade ult: +8% charge
When Hulk's HP is depleted, he reverts to Bruce Banner — the lowest HP form in the entire game. This is a death sentence if you stay in it. The goal is to transform back into Hulk as fast as possible: shoot enemies with Banner's gun (headshots give more charge), throw a Gamma Grenade at your own feet (+8% ult charge), and hit enemies with grenades for additional charge. [[3]](#__3)
⚠️ Banner Priority: The moment you enter Banner form, your only goal is to get out of it. Throw a Gamma Grenade at your feet immediately for the +8% ult charge, then run to cover or your healers. Do NOT try to fight in Banner form — you will die. [[3]](#__3)
Q — ULT
HULK SMASH!
Ult HP: 1500
Clap CD: 1s
Lockdown CD: 5s
Kills support: 3 claps
Hulk transforms into his most powerful form with 1500 HP. Heavy Blow now knocks enemies back, Gamma Burst has a 1-second cooldown (vs. 8s normally), and Radioactive Lockdown refreshes every 5 seconds. Also unlocks World Breaker — a second ultimate within the ult form. Two primary uses: create space by knocking enemies off objectives with Heavy Blow knockback, or spam Gamma Bursts at the enemy backline (3 hits kills most supports). [[3]](#__3)
⚡ Ult Charge Tech: Gamma Shield absorbs 100% of damage as ult charge. In a heavy teamfight, shielding a burst ult can give you 40–60% ult charge instantly. Combine with Banner's self-grenade (+8%) and consistent Gamma Burst hits to charge HULK SMASH faster than almost any other tank ult in the game. [[3]](#__3)

Advanced Techniques

Combo Guide

Hulk's combos revolve around Incredible Leap timing and the Lockdown → Gamma Burst kill setup. The jump tech is the most important mechanical skill — master it before anything else. [[3]](#__3)

Brawl Frontline

  • 1
    Enable tap-to-charge jump in settings Pre-match
    Go to settings and change Incredible Leap to tap-to-charge mode. This lets you have your jump pre-charged at all times — critical for escaping dangerous situations instantly. [[3]](#__3)
  • 2
    Engage enemy frontline with Heavy Blow LMB 0.0s
    Walk into the enemy frontline and punch. The wide radius means you'll hit multiple targets even without precise aim. Focus on the highest-threat enemy first. [[3]](#__3)
  • 3
    Raise Gamma Shield on burst abilities RMB Reactive
    The moment you see a burst ult wind up (Jeff, Iron Man, Thing), raise your shield immediately. You'll absorb the entire ult and convert it to your own ult charge. [[3]](#__3)
  • 4
    Fire Gamma Burst at backline through tanks F Every 8s
    Gamma Burst pierces through frontline tanks — use it to pressure the enemy backline even while brawling at the frontline. Don't save it; use it on cooldown. [[3]](#__3)
  • 5
    Leap to health pack if low HP SHIFT Any
    Hulk has no self-sustain — when low, leap to a health pack or your healers immediately. Don't try to fight through low HP; Banner form is a death sentence. [[3]](#__3)
Brawl principle: Your job at the frontline is to absorb burst ults with Gamma Shield and convert them to your own ult charge. Every burst ult you shield is a massive swing in your team's favor — you negate their ult AND accelerate yours. [[3]](#__3)

Lockdown → Gamma Burst Kill

Hulk's most reliable kill combo — the stun prevents escape and the 70-damage Gamma Burst finishes most supports. [[3]](#__3)

  • 1
    Identify low-HP fleeing target 0.0s
    Look for a support or DPS below 70 HP trying to escape. This combo is wasted on full-HP targets — save it for the finish. [[3]](#__3)
  • 2
    Confirm no allies are actively shooting the target 0.0s
    Critical check: if your DPS is currently shooting the target, do NOT stun them — the exile makes them immune to ally damage. Only stun when you're the one finishing. [[3]](#__3)
  • 3
    Radioactive Lockdown E ~0.2s
    Stun the fleeing target. They're now frozen in place and cannot dodge your follow-up. The exile window is short — act immediately. [[3]](#__3)
  • 4
    Gamma Burst immediately F ~0.4s
    Fire Gamma Burst the moment the stun lands. The 70 damage kills most supports (250 HP) if they were already below 80 HP. Don't hesitate — the stun window is brief. [[3]](#__3)
Why it works: The stun removes the target's only defense — movement. A stunned support cannot dodge Gamma Burst's projectile. This combo reliably secures kills that would otherwise escape. [[3]](#__3)

Dive Leap Combo Hard

Hulk's signature high-skill play — crash into the enemy backline, disrupt, and escape before the team can respond. Requires mastered jump tech. [[3]](#__3)

  • 1
    Pre-charge Incredible Leap SHIFT (tap)
    With tap-to-charge enabled, tap SHIFT to pre-charge your jump. You can now move normally with the jump ready to fire at any moment. [[3]](#__3)
  • 2
    Identify isolated backline target
    Look for a support or DPS separated from their tanks. Don't dive into a cluster — you need a clear target and a clear escape route. [[3]](#__3)
  • 3
    Release leap onto target SHIFT (release) ~0.3s
    Release the pre-charged leap directly onto your target. The speed of the dive gives them minimal reaction time. [[3]](#__3)
  • 4
    Heavy Blow + Gamma Burst on landing LMB → F 0.3–1.5s
    Punch immediately on landing, then fire Gamma Burst. If the target is a support, 2–3 punches + a Gamma Burst is enough to kill them. [[3]](#__3)
  • 5
    Pre-charge escape leap immediately SHIFT (tap) <2.0s
    The moment you've dealt damage, pre-charge your next leap. Don't wait until you're low HP — escape before the enemy team can respond. Timing is everything. [[3]](#__3)
  • 6
    Escape to cover or healers SHIFT (release) <2.5s
    Leap back to your team or a health pack. The entire dive-and-escape sequence should take under 2.5 seconds — if you're staying longer, you're overextending. [[3]](#__3)
The golden rule of Dive Hulk: Get in, disrupt, get out. You are NOT trying to win a 1v5 in the enemy backline. Your goal is to force 2–3 enemies to deal with you for 2 seconds — that's enough to win the fight for your team. [[3]](#__3)
