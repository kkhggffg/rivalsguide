Groot
Complete Guide

The Flora Colossus — a Vanguard who reshapes the battlefield with deployable wooden walls, roots enemies in place, and transforms into a massive damage-absorbing giant with his ultimate.

If you want a tank who controls the entire map geometry, creates safe zones for your team out of nothing, and can single-handedly shut down sniper sightlines that no other Vanguard can block — Groot is the most strategically deep tank in Marvel Rivals.

Groot is rated S-Tier in the current meta — the highest-rated Vanguard on most tier lists — because his wall mechanic provides a form of map control that no other hero in the game can replicate. His skill expression is entirely in wall placement: knowing which sightlines to block, where to create safe zones for supports, and when to use Strangling Prison to lock down a key target. A Groot who places walls correctly wins fights before they start.

Kit Breakdown

Abilities & Skills

PASSIVE
Photosynthesis
Groot passively regenerates health when standing still or moving slowly — representing his natural regenerative biology. The regen rate is modest but meaningful during low-intensity phases between fights. More importantly, this passive synergizes with wall placement: when Groot places a wall and steps back to let it absorb damage, he's simultaneously regenerating HP while the wall does the work. It rewards a patient, positional playstyle over aggressive frontlining.
💡 After placing walls at a choke point, step back 5–8 meters and let the walls absorb the enemy's damage. While they're shooting your walls, you're regenerating HP and your supports are healing you from behind. This passive sustain loop is what makes Groot so difficult to burst down in extended fights.
LMB
Vine Strike
Groot lashes out with his wooden arms in a short-range melee strike, dealing damage to all enemies in a frontal arc. The attack has a wide horizontal sweep that can hit multiple enemies simultaneously. Despite being melee range, the arc width means enemies slightly to the side of Groot still take full damage. Each hit also contributes to his ult charge meter, making consistent primary fire important even when walls are doing the heavy lifting.
Damage: ~48 per hit DPS: ~95/s Type: Melee arc Range: ~4m Multi-hit: Yes
💡 Vine Strike's arc width is wider than it looks. Position yourself at the corner of a wall so enemies rounding the corner walk directly into the arc — they take damage before they can react. This corner-trap technique is one of Groot's most effective close-range plays.
RMB
Thornlash Wall
Groot grows a large wooden wall at a targeted location. The wall has 800 HP, blocks all projectiles and line of sight, and can be oriented in any direction before placement. Up to 3 walls can be active simultaneously — when a 4th is placed, the oldest wall is automatically destroyed. Walls also function as platforms: allies can jump on top of them to gain elevated firing positions. This is the most strategically complex ability in the entire game.
Wall HP: 800 each Max active: 3 Orientation: Fully adjustable Platform: Yes — allies can stand on top Cooldown: ~8s per charge
💡 The three most impactful wall placements in any match: (1) blocking the main sniper sightline on the map, (2) creating a safe healing pocket directly behind your frontline for supports, and (3) placing a wall perpendicular to a choke point to force enemies into a narrow angle where your team can focus them one at a time.
SHIFT
Spore Bomb
Groot launches a spore projectile that explodes on impact, dealing AoE damage and slowing all enemies caught in the blast radius. The slow significantly reduces enemy movement speed for several seconds, making it easier for teammates to land abilities and harder for enemies to escape engagements. The AoE radius is generous — it doesn't require precise aim to be effective. Primarily used to set up Strangling Prison roots or to slow diving enemies before they reach your supports.
Damage: ~60 AoE Slow: ~30% movement Duration: ~2s Cooldown: ~10s Radius: ~5m
💡 Use Spore Bomb on a diving hero the moment they commit — the slow reduces their movement speed enough that your supports can reposition and your DPS can land follow-up damage. Against Black Panther and Spider-Man specifically, a well-timed Spore Bomb turns their dive from a guaranteed kill into a failed engage.
E
Strangling Prison
Groot sends out a network of vines that root all enemies in the target area for 1.5 seconds — preventing all movement while still allowing them to attack. The root affects multiple enemies simultaneously, making it the best multi-target CC ability of any Vanguard. Rooted enemies cannot dodge, jump, or reposition — they become stationary targets for your entire team. The 1.5-second duration is long enough for most DPS heroes to land a full damage rotation on the rooted target.
Root duration: 1.5s Targets: Multiple (AoE) Cooldown: ~14s Range: ~8m Breaks on: Duration only
💡 Strangling Prison is Scarlet Witch's best friend — a rooted enemy cannot dodge or interrupt Reality Erasure's 4-second charge. Coordinate with your Scarlet Witch: Groot roots the enemy team, Wanda charges her ult safely during the 1.5-second window. This two-hero combination produces one of the highest burst damage plays in the game.
Q — ULT
Flora Colossus
Groot transforms into a massive Flora Colossus — dramatically increasing his size, maximum HP, damage output, and the size of all his abilities. In Colossus form, Thornlash Walls become enormous, Vine Strike hits in a much wider arc, and Strangling Prison covers a significantly larger area. The transformation lasts approximately 10 seconds. During this window, Groot becomes the most durable and threatening frontliner in the game — his increased size also makes him a larger target, but the HP increase more than compensates.
Duration: ~10s HP increase: Significant Size: Massively increased All abilities: Enhanced AoE
💡 Activate Flora Colossus when pushing into a contested objective — not when defending. The massive size increase makes you the most threatening frontline presence in the game during the 10-second window, and the enhanced Strangling Prison can root the entire enemy team simultaneously. Push immediately after activation — don't wait for the perfect moment.
TEAM-UP
Groot's Sanctuary — with Rocket Raccoon
When paired with Rocket Raccoon, Groot gains access to Groot's Sanctuary — a team-up enhancement where Rocket can ride on Groot's shoulder, gaining a mobile elevated firing platform while Groot benefits from Rocket's repair and support capabilities. Rocket's healing and ammo support directly addresses Groot's zero self-sustain weakness, while Groot's walls and large body provide Rocket with the safest possible position to heal from in the entire game.
💡 In a Groot + Rocket composition, have Rocket ride Groot's shoulder during Flora Colossus. Rocket fires from the elevated position on a giant Groot who is nearly unkillable during the ult window — this combination is one of the most durable frontline pairings in Marvel Rivals. Enemies must choose between shooting the enormous Groot or the tiny Rocket on his shoulder.

Heroes who can fly or wall-jump over Groot's walls (Iron Man, Storm) bypass his terrain control entirely. Groot has no answer for vertical mobility.
🥊
Close-Range Burst Heroes
Groot's large hitbox and slow movement make him easy to burst at close range. Psylocke and similar heroes can delete him before Strangling Prison activates.

Sightline Block + Hold

  • 1
    Identify the main sniper sightline before the fight starts pre-fight
    Every map in Marvel Rivals has 1–2 dominant long-range sightlines that snipers use. Learn them. Groot's first wall placement should always block the most dangerous one before your team advances.
  • 2
    Place Wall perpendicular to the sightline RMB 0.0s
    Orient the wall so it fully blocks the sniper's line of fire. A correctly placed wall makes the entire sightline unusable for its full 800 HP lifespan — forcing the sniper to reposition entirely.
  • 3
    Place second wall to create a safe healing corridor RMB
    Behind the first wall, place a second wall parallel to your team's movement direction — creating a protected lane your supports can heal from without exposure. This is the "safe pocket" that makes Groot's supports nearly unkillable.
  • 4
    Hold the third wall charge for reactive placement reserve
    Never deploy all 3 walls immediately. Keep one charge in reserve to reactively block a flank, cover a retreating ally, or replace a destroyed wall.
Pro tip: The single highest-impact wall placement in the game is blocking the main sniper sightline on the first tick of the round — before the enemy sniper even reaches their position. A Groot who pre-places walls during the approach phase wins the map geometry battle before the fight begins.

Spore Bomb → Strangling Prison

  • 1
    Identify a high-value target to lock down pre-engage
    Prioritize enemy supports and high-mobility Duelists. A rooted Loki or Scarlet Witch is a guaranteed kill for your DPS. A rooted Black Panther mid-dive saves your entire backline.
  • 2
    Land Spore Bomb on the target SHIFT 0.0s
    The slow from Spore Bomb reduces the target's movement speed by ~30% for 2 seconds — making them significantly easier to hit with Strangling Prison's follow-up root. Against fast heroes, the slow is mandatory for the root to connect reliably.
  • 3
    Immediately follow with Strangling Prison E ~0.3s
    The slowed target cannot dodge the root. They are now stationary for 1.5 seconds — long enough for any DPS hero to land a full damage rotation. Call out the root to your team immediately.
  • 4
    Follow up with Vine Strike while target is rooted LMB
    Close the distance during the root window and land Vine Strike arcs on the stationary target. Even Groot's modest DPS becomes threatening against a hero who literally cannot move for 1.5 seconds.
Why it works: Spore Bomb's slow removes the primary reason Strangling Prison misses — target movement. Against a slowed hero, the root connects nearly 100% of the time. The 1.5-second root window is long enough for Scarlet Witch to charge Reality Erasure halfway, for Winter Soldier to land Kraken Impact, or for any burst DPS to secure a kill.

Wall Platform Elevation Play

  • 1
    Identify a DPS hero who benefits from elevation pre-fight
    Star-Lord, Winter Soldier, and any ranged DPS benefit enormously from elevated positions. Identify which ally would gain the most from a platform above the standard ground level.
  • 2
    Place wall horizontally at mid-height as a platform RMB
    Orient the wall flat rather than vertical — creating a raised platform your ally can jump onto. Placed against an existing wall or structure, this creates an elevated firing position that didn't exist on the map before.
  • 3
    Signal your DPS ally to use the platform immediately
    Communication is essential for this technique. Ping the wall or use voice chat — a platform that no ally uses is a wasted wall charge. In coordinated play, pre-designate which ally will use elevation platforms before the fight.
  • 4
    Place a second wall vertically to protect the elevated ally RMB
    A DPS hero on an elevated platform is exposed from the front. Place a second wall vertically in front of the platform — your ally now has both height advantage and frontal cover simultaneously.
Why it works: Walls as platforms create elevated positions on maps that have none — fundamentally changing the geometry of a fight. An enemy team that prepared for a ground-level engagement suddenly has a Star-Lord firing from 5 meters above their heads with no natural cover to hide behind.

Flora Colossus Full Push Expert

Coordinating Flora Colossus with your team for a full objective push — maximizing every second of the 10-second transformation window.

  • 1
    Communicate ult timing with your team before activation
    Flora Colossus is a push tool, not a defensive tool. Your entire team needs to be ready to advance the moment you transform — a Colossus Groot standing still while teammates are out of position wastes the entire window.
  • 2
    Activate Flora Colossus at the objective entrance Q
    Transform at the chokepoint leading to the objective — not already on the objective. The transformation animation and size increase clears the chokepoint, and your team follows immediately behind you.
  • 3
    Use enhanced Strangling Prison immediately on grouped enemies E 0–2s
    In Colossus form, Strangling Prison covers a dramatically larger area. Use it immediately on the grouped defenders — the enhanced root catches multiple enemies simultaneously, giving your team a free damage window on stationary targets.
  • 4
    Place enhanced walls to cut off enemy retreat and reinforcement RMB 2–6s
    Colossus walls are enormous — place them behind the enemy team to cut off their retreat to spawn and block reinforcements from reaching the objective. Enemies are now trapped between your team and a wall they cannot quickly destroy.
  • 5
    Contest the objective for the full 10-second window 0–10s
    Stay on the objective for the entire transformation duration. Your massive HP pool and enhanced abilities make you nearly unkillable for 10 seconds — that's enough time to capture most objectives or push the payload a significant distance.
Why it works: Flora Colossus transforms Groot from a terrain-control tank into an unstoppable objective machine. The enhanced Strangling Prison roots the entire defending team, the enormous walls cut off reinforcements, and the HP increase means the enemy team cannot burst Groot down before the objective is captured. In coordinated play, a well-timed Flora Colossus push wins rounds outright.
Common Questions

FAQ

Groot is medium difficulty — his primary fire and basic tanking are straightforward, but his skill expression is entirely in wall placement. A Groot who places walls randomly is a mediocre tank. A Groot who places walls to block the main sniper sightline, create support pockets, and set up elevation platforms is the most impactful hero in the game. Start by learning one wall placement per map, then gradually add the second and third placements as you gain experience.

Three placements cover 90% of situations: (1) block the main sniper sightline before your team advances, (2) create a protected healing corridor behind your frontline for supports, and (3) place a wall perpendicular to the main choke point to force enemies into a narrow angle. Never place all 3 walls at once — always keep one charge in reserve for reactive placement when a flank opens or a wall gets destroyed.

You can't fully prevent it, but you can make it costly. Place walls at angles that force enemies to reposition to shoot them — a wall they have to walk around to destroy is far more durable than one directly in their line of fire. Also, when a wall is destroyed, immediately redeploy it on cooldown. Enemies who spent 3 seconds destroying your wall just gave your team 3 seconds of free positioning — and now the wall is back.

Flora Colossus is a push ultimate — activate it when your team is ready to advance onto an objective, not when defending. The 10-second window is most valuable when you're pushing into a contested point with your full team behind you. Never activate it defensively or when your team is spread out — a Colossus Groot with no teammates nearby is just a large target with no support.

Coordinated wall destruction is Groot's hardest counter — if the entire enemy team focuses fire on his walls simultaneously, all 3 can be destroyed in seconds, removing his entire kit. Heroes with vertical mobility (Iron Man, Storm) bypass walls entirely by flying over them. If you're playing against Groot, never let his walls stand unchallenged — assign one DPS to destroy walls on cooldown, and the rest of Groot's kit becomes significantly less threatening.
