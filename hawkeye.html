Hawkeye
Complete Guide

Clint Barton — the only hero in Marvel Rivals who can one-shot every Strategist and Duelist in the game (except Wolverine and Mister Fantastic). Where he lacks superpowers, he makes up for in pure lethality and unmatched LoS control.

A great Hawkeye player is either carrying or throwing — there's no middle ground. Land your headshots and you single-handedly force the entire enemy team to play around your sightlines. Miss them and you're a liability. The archer who wins teamfights with a single arrow. [[0]](#__0) [[2]](#__2)

Hawkeye has 250 HP — same as most supports — making him fragile. His Piercing Arrow scales from 28 damage (uncharged) to 70 damage (fully charged at 0.9s), with critical hits on top. He has infinite ammo, meaning sustained pressure is always available. The key distinction from Black Widow: Hawkeye can one-shot every Strategist and Duelist in the game without any damage boost — he is the only hero with this property. His projectile speed also scales with charge: 120 m/s uncharged to 180 m/s at full charge.

Hawkeye's primary weapon — a charged bow shot that scales from 28 damage (instant release) to 70 damage (0.9s full charge). Both damage and projectile speed scale with charge time. The 0.9s window is short enough to charge behind cover without exposing yourself.

Hawkeye activates Hunter's Sight, marking visible enemies and firing enhanced arrows with increased damage and tracking. The ult's tracking is only effective against exposed targets. Vanguards should body-block for their backline the moment they hear the ult activation.

Chokepoint Blast Arrow Loop

  • 1
    Identify the enemy's primary advance route Pre-fight
    Every map has 1–2 chokepoints enemies must pass through. Position yourself with a clear sightline to that chokepoint before the fight starts.
  • 2
    Fire Blast Arrow at chokepoint entrance E 0.0s
    As enemies approach, fire a Blast Arrow at the chokepoint entrance. The AoE punishes grouped enemies and forces them to spread out or slow their advance.
  • 3
    Charge Piercing Arrow behind cover LMB (hold) 0.0–0.9s
    While Blast Arrow is on cooldown, charge your Piercing Arrow behind cover. The -20% movement penalty doesn't matter if you're not moving.
  • 4
    Peek and headshot the most exposed target LMB (release) ~1.0s
    Peek from cover with a fully charged arrow and aim for the head of the most exposed enemy — ideally a support who stepped out to heal. Return to cover immediately after firing.
  • 5
    Repeat — never stay in the same spot Loop
    Rotate to a new angle after 2–3 shots. Enemies will start focusing your position — unpredictability keeps you alive.
Chokepoint principle: Hawkeye's infinite ammo means he never runs out of pressure. Enemies advancing through a chokepoint must either take Blast Arrow AoE damage, slow down and give your team time, or find a longer route that splits their team. All three outcomes benefit you. [[0]](#__0)

Peek → Full Charge Headshot

Hawkeye's core kill pattern — charge behind cover, peek to fire, return to cover. The foundation of all high-level Hawkeye play.

  • 1
    Position on off-angle high ground Pre-engage
    Find a position that gives you a sightline to the enemy backline from an unexpected angle. Off-angles mean targets aren't actively dodging your shots — the single biggest factor in landing headshots.
  • 2
    Begin charging behind cover LMB (hold) 0.0–0.9s
    Start charging your arrow while still behind cover. The full charge takes 0.9 seconds — time this so you're ready to fire the instant you peek.
  • 3
    Peek, aim head, release LMB (release) ~0.9s
    Step out from cover with a fully charged arrow already ready. Aim for the head and release. The entire peek window should be under 0.5 seconds — charge behind cover, peek to shoot, return immediately.
  • 4
    Return to cover and begin next charge Immediate
    Don't admire your shot — return to cover and start charging the next arrow immediately. The rhythm is: charge → peek → fire → cover → charge.
The pace principle (Reddit community tip): "Pace yourself and keep a nice, steady and slow pace. Stressing and being rigid is your enemy. Hawkeye is not about reaction, it's about prediction." — The best Hawkeye players are calm, methodical, and always one step ahead of enemy movement.

Movement-Lock Prediction Shot Hard

Hawkeye's highest-skill technique — shooting targets during movement-locking abilities when they cannot dodge.

  • 1
    Learn each hero's movement-locking abilities Knowledge
    Every hero has at least one ability that locks them into a fixed trajectory: Spider-Man's web swing, dive abilities, leap attacks, and charge moves. During these animations, the target cannot change direction.
  • 2
    Watch for the ability animation start Reactive
    The moment you see a target begin a movement-locking ability, start charging your arrow. You have the full animation duration to charge and aim.
  • 3
    Lead your shot to their destination LMB (release)
    Aim where the target will be at the end of their movement, not where they are now. Hawkeye's arrows have travel time — lead the shot by the distance they'll cover during flight.
  • 4
    Spider-Man example: aim at web-swing landing spot
    When Spider-Man uses Get Over Here or Amazing Combo, he locks into a swing arc. Aim at the point where he'll land — fire a full-charge arrow there and he'll walk into it.
Why this works: Most players try to track moving targets in real time — this is extremely difficult with Hawkeye's slow projectile speed. Prediction shooting removes the tracking challenge entirely: you're aiming at a fixed point, not a moving one. Master the movement patterns of the 5–6 most common heroes and this becomes second nature.

FAQ

The key difference: Hawkeye can one-shot every Strategist and Duelist without any damage boost. Black Widow cannot one-shot anyone without Mantis. Hawkeye is also projectile-based (travel time, arc) while Black Widow is hitscan (instant). Hawkeye has infinite ammo and better sustained pressure; Black Widow has zero falloff and a melee kit. Hawkeye is B-Tier; Black Widow is D-Tier. For most players, Hawkeye is the better pick.

Always aim for a full 0.9-second charge before firing. Anything below 0.7 seconds is below minimum charge and deals only 28 damage — not enough to kill anyone. The full 0.9s charge gives you 70 damage plus critical hit potential, which is the one-shot threshold for Strategists and Duelists. Charge behind cover so the 0.9s window doesn't expose you.

An off-angle is any position that gives you a sightline to the enemy that they don't expect — typically from the side or behind their shields. Off-angles matter because targets who don't see you aren't actively dodging your arrows, making headshots dramatically easier. They also split the enemy team's focus: they must address both your frontline and your off-angle simultaneously. Always look for off-angles before the fight starts, not during it.

Divers are Hawkeye's hardest counter. The counter-play: (1) position near your tanks so they can peel; (2) use Crescent Slash + double jump to create distance when a diver reaches you; (3) ping the diver immediately so your team responds; (4) most importantly — prevent the dive by positioning in spots that are hard to reach. High ground with limited approach routes is your best defense.

Use Hunter's Sight in two situations: (1) as a teamfight opener when 3+ enemies are pushing through a chokepoint in the open — the enhanced arrows can wipe an entire backline; (2) as a finisher when multiple enemies are low HP and hiding behind cover. Never use it when enemies have terrain cover or when their Vanguard is body-blocking — the ult's tracking is only effective against exposed targets.

Start by learning the 5 most common movement-locking abilities: Spider-Man's web swing, Black Panther's dash, Iron Fist's leap, Captain America's shield throw, and Hulk's Incredible Leap. In practice, spend 10 minutes in a custom game just watching these animations and firing arrows at the landing spots — not at the heroes themselves. Once you can consistently hit stationary targets at the landing point, start doing it in real matches. The community consensus: prediction shooting is a learnable skill, not a talent.
