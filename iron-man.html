Iron Man
Complete Guide

Tony Stark — the genius billionaire turned aerial powerhouse of Marvel Rivals. Dominant at range, devastating with Unibeam, and nearly untouchable when played from altitude.

If you want a ranged DPS who rewards game sense over mechanical skill — and can single-handedly delete backlines from the sky — Iron Man is built for you.

Iron Man is a superb ranged duelist who dominates from altitude. His 250 HP is low for a full-armor hero, but his aerial mobility and long-range burst kit mean he rarely needs to take close-range fights. His power comes from positioning — stay high, stay mobile, and punish grounded enemies who can't reach you.

Kit Breakdown

Abilities & Skills

PASSIVE
Powered Armor
Tony's suit provides constant flight capability. Unlike other mobile heroes, Iron Man can hover indefinitely at any altitude — he is never forced to land. This passive defines his entire playstyle.
💡 Always maintain altitude above 15m. Most heroes can't reach you, and you gain full vision of the battlefield to pick your targets.
LMB
Repulsor Blast
Fires a repulsor energy bolt dealing 55 damage per hit. Fast projectile with excellent range. Your primary sustained damage tool — reliable, consistent, and safe to use from altitude.
💡 Aim for stationary or slow targets first. Repulsor Blast rewards patience — don't spam it; fire when you have a clear line of sight.
RMB
Repulsor Barrage
Rapid-fire mode that unleashes a burst of smaller repulsor shots. Lower individual damage but high total output at close-to-medium range. Best used when targets are immobilized or grouped.
💡 Switch to Barrage immediately after landing a Unibeam stun — the target can't dodge and you maximize damage during their stagger window.
SHIFT
Hyper Velocity
Activates a speed boost that dramatically increases Iron Man's flight speed in a chosen direction. Primary escape and repositioning tool. Can be used to dodge incoming projectiles or rapidly close distance for a Unibeam.
💡 Use Hyper Velocity to dodge Spider-Man's Web Pull — the moment you see the web projectile, boost perpendicular to its trajectory.
E
Unibeam
Charges and fires a devastating energy beam from the chest reactor. Deals massive burst damage and briefly staggers targets hit. The centerpiece of Iron Man's kit — his highest single-hit damage ability. Requires a short charge time, during which Iron Man is slowed.
💡 Always pre-position before charging. Find a high angle, lock onto an isolated support, charge Unibeam, then fire. Never charge it in the open at ground level.
Q — ULT
Invincible Iron Man
Tony upgrades to the most advanced armor configuration, gaining dramatically increased damage, faster Unibeam charge, enhanced flight speed, and temporary damage resistance. Lasts 8 seconds. Transforms Iron Man from a poke threat into a full assassination machine.
💡 Activate during a teamfight when 2–3 enemies are grouped and below 60% HP. The reduced Unibeam charge time lets you fire twice in the 8-second window — that's enough to delete an entire backline.
Matchup Analysis

Counters & Matchups

✅ Iron Man Counters
🟢
Supports (General)
Iron Man's aerial angle bypasses most support positioning. Unibeam one-shots or near-one-shots most healers from above.
🛡
Hulk
Hulk is a large, slow target with no ranged response. Iron Man can safely poke him down from altitude without ever entering melee range.
🏹
Hawkeye
Hawkeye's arrows are slow enough to dodge at altitude. Iron Man out-ranges and out-bursts him in most aerial matchups.
❌ Hard Counters Iron Man
🕷
Spider-Man
Web Pull yanks Iron Man out of the sky mid-Unibeam charge, cancelling the ability entirely. Spider-Man's hardest counter in the game.
🧲
Magneto
Magnetic fields intercept Repulsor Blasts and disrupt flight paths. Magneto can force Iron Man into unfavorable ground-level fights.
🌙
Luna Snow
Luna's abilities can disrupt Iron Man's tech and her freeze effects punish the slow charge animation of Unibeam at close range.

Advanced Techniques

Combo Guide

Iron Man's combos are less about button timing and more about positioning and pre-charging. Pick your difficulty and start there:

Standard Aerial Burst

  • 1
    Gain altitude PASSIVE 0.0s
    Fly to at least 15–20m above the battlefield. This puts you out of melee reach and gives you full vision of enemy positioning.
  • 2
    Identify isolated target 0.0–2.0s
    Look for supports or low-HP heroes separated from their tanks. Never open on a full-HP tank — you'll waste your burst and expose yourself.
  • 3
    Poke with Repulsor Blasts LMB 2.0–4.0s
    Chip the target down to ~60% HP with Repulsor Blasts. This sets up a Unibeam kill rather than a Unibeam opener — much safer.
  • 4
    Fire Unibeam to finish E 4.0–5.5s
    Once the target is weakened, charge and release Unibeam for the kill. At 60% HP, most supports die to a single Unibeam hit.
  • 5
    Reposition immediately SHIFT <6.0s
    After firing, use Hyper Velocity to change altitude and angle. Never stay in the same position after a Unibeam — enemies will have tracked your location.
Pro tip: The poke-then-finish approach is safer than opening with Unibeam. If you miss a cold Unibeam opener, you've wasted your best ability and you're now slow and exposed.

Unibeam Dive Combo

  • 1
    Pre-charge Unibeam at altitude E 0.0s
    Begin charging Unibeam while still high up and out of sight. The charge animation is invisible to enemies until you descend into their view.
  • 2
    Dive into range SHIFT ~0.8s
    Use Hyper Velocity to rapidly close distance while the Unibeam is still charging. You appear in the enemy's vision already at near-full charge.
  • 3
    Release Unibeam on target E ~1.2s
    Fire the fully charged beam. The target has less than 0.5 seconds to react from the moment they see you — most players can't dodge it.
  • 4
    Follow up with Repulsor Barrage RMB 1.2–2.5s
    If Unibeam didn't kill, immediately switch to Barrage during the stagger window. Most supports are dead before the stagger ends.
  • 5
    Escape back to altitude SHIFT <3.0s
    Boost straight up after the kill. You're at your most vulnerable at ground level — get back to altitude before the enemy team can respond.
Why it works: Pre-charging at altitude removes the slow charge animation from the enemy's reaction window. By the time they see Iron Man, the beam is already firing.

Ultimate Deletion Combo Hard

Use Invincible Iron Man to chain two Unibeam kills within the 8-second window — this is Iron Man's highest-impact play.

  • 1
    Wait for teamfight to develop
    Don't pop ultimate immediately. Wait until 2–3 enemies are below 60% HP and grouped within a 10m radius. The ultimate is wasted on full-HP tanks.
  • 2
    Activate Invincible Iron Man Q
    Pop the ultimate at altitude before diving. The enhanced state is active before you enter the enemy's view.
  • 3
    First Unibeam on priority target E
    Charge time is dramatically reduced during ultimate. Fire on the highest-threat target — usually the enemy support or a low-HP DPS.
  • 4
    Reposition and charge second Unibeam E
    The reduced cooldown during ultimate lets you fire a second Unibeam within the 8-second window. Chain dive → kill → reposition → kill.

Hyper Velocity Cancel Expert

Advanced tech that uses Hyper Velocity to cancel the Unibeam's slow animation window — making Iron Man nearly impossible to punish during his most vulnerable moment.

  • 1
    Begin Unibeam charge E
    Start charging at altitude. You're slow during this window — this is when Spider-Man and other dive heroes will try to punish you.
  • 2
    Detect incoming threat
    Watch for Spider-Man's web projectile, Hawkeye's arrows, or any dive hero closing distance. You have a ~0.4 second window to react.
  • 3
    Hyper Velocity perpendicular SHIFT
    Boost sideways or upward — not backward. Perpendicular movement is hardest to track and maintains your charge progress while breaking the threat's aim.
  • 4
    Complete charge and fire from new position E
    You've dodged the threat AND maintained your Unibeam charge. Fire from the new angle — the target is now out of position trying to track you.
Why it works: Most players expect Iron Man to be a static target while charging Unibeam. The Hyper Velocity cancel breaks that assumption — you're mobile during your most powerful ability, which almost no one accounts for.
