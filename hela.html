Hela
Complete Guide

The Goddess of Death — who dominates long-range engagements with hitscan Nightsword Thorns, stuns advancing enemies with Soul Drainer, and transforms into a crow to escape death before calling upon her ultimate to haunt the entire battlefield.

If you want the single best long-range Duelist in Marvel Rivals — a hero who can two-tap 275 HP heroes in under one second, shut down every flying hero in the game, and survive dives with multiple layered defensive tools — Hela is the Goddess of Death for a reason.

Hela is S-Tier and has dominated every version of Marvel Rivals since the beta — the only hero to maintain top-tier status across all seasons. Her primary fire is the best long-range damage tool in the game, her anti-air capability is unmatched, and her defensive kit is surprisingly robust for a sniper. The caveat is absolute: if you cannot land consistent headshots, Hela is a throw pick. Her entire power budget is concentrated in headshot damage — body-shot Hela deals less effective damage than most other Duelists. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)

Kit Breakdown

Abilities & Skills

PASSIVE
Goddess of Death
When Hela activates her ultimate, she immediately gains 1000 temporary HP on top of her base 250 HP — giving her an effective 1250 HP pool during the ult window. This passive-ult interaction means activating Goddess of Death just before dying effectively resurrects her with a massive HP buffer, allowing her to continue fighting through situations that would eliminate any other Duelist. The temporary HP decays after the ult ends. [[0]](#__0)
💡 The most important Hela skill to master: learn to activate Goddess of Death reactively when you're being burst down. A Hela at 30 HP who activates her ult instantly becomes a 1030 HP target — enemies who committed to the kill are suddenly fighting a completely different opponent. [[0]](#__0)
LMB
Nightsword Thorn
Hela throws a single Nightsword Thorn as a direct-hit hitscan projectile dealing 70 body damage or 140 on headshot. The fire rate is 2 rounds per second with 10 ammo per magazine. Damage falloff begins at 18 meters and reduces to 70% at 30 meters — meaning within 18 meters, two headshots eliminate any 275 HP hero in exactly 1 second. The half-second delay between shots gives her enough time to jiggle peek corners between each throw. [[0]](#__0)
Body damage: 70 Headshot: 140 Fire rate: 2/s Ammo: 10 Falloff start: 18m Falloff max: 70% at 30m
💡 Prioritize the enemy backline — supports and Duelists, not Vanguards. Hela's headshot two-tap is wasted on tanks who require 4+ shots to kill. Target the squishiest enemy regardless of how far back they're positioned — her effective range reaches further than almost any other hero in the game. [[0]](#__0) [[2]](#__2)
RMB
Piercing Night
Hela fires 4 Nightsword Thorns simultaneously in a spread pattern. Each projectile deals 10 damage on hit and sticks to enemies. After a 3-second delay, each stuck projectile explodes for 35 damage in a 3-meter spherical radius. The Season 2 spell field damage buff enables a full 4-projectile hit plus a single primary fire body shot to eliminate 250 HP heroes outright. Against Vanguards, their large hitboxes make it easy to land all 4 projectiles for a reliable burst of damage. [[0]](#__0)
Projectiles: 4 Hit damage: 10 each Explosion dmg: 35 each Explosion delay: 3s Cooldown: 8s Speed: 80m/s
💡 The anti-dive combo: when a melee Duelist flanks you, immediately stick them with Piercing Night, then use Astral Flock to escape. By the time you transform back, the 3-second explosion timer detonates — dealing 140 explosion damage while you resume primary fire. This combo turns a dangerous dive into a free elimination. [[0]](#__0)
SHIFT
Astral Flock
Hela transforms into a Nastrond Crow and dashes 18 meters at 15 m/s, gaining complete invincibility during the transformation. She can press SHIFT again to cancel the transformation early. The 15-second cooldown is the longest of any of her abilities — making every use a high-stakes decision. During the dash she cannot attack, but she is completely immune to all damage, stuns, and ultimates. [[0]](#__0)
Dash distance: 18m Dash speed: 15m/s Invincible: Yes Cooldown: 15s
💡 Save Astral Flock exclusively for survival — never use it for aggressive repositioning. When enemies see you use it, they know they have a 15-second window to dive and eliminate you. When Astral Flock is on cooldown, position near cover, a health pack, or an ally who can protect you. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)
E
Soul Drainer
Hela projects an explosive Hel sphere in an arced trajectory that detonates on impact, dealing 40 damage in a 3-meter radius and stunning all enemies hit for 0.3 seconds. The stun duration is short but provides just enough time to line up a critical headshot. The 12-second cooldown means it must be used deliberately — either defensively to interrupt a dive, or offensively to stun a grouped cluster for a guaranteed headshot follow-up. [[0]](#__0)
Damage: 40 (AoE) Stun: 0.3s Radius: 3m Cooldown: 12s Speed: 40m/s
💡 Soul Drainer's primary value is defensive — use it to interrupt advancing Vanguards or flanking Duelists. The 0.3-second stun is enough to land a guaranteed headshot follow-up. Against grouped enemies, the 3-meter AoE can stun multiple targets simultaneously, creating a brief window to two-tap the lowest HP target. [[0]](#__0) [[2]](#__2)
Q — ULT
Goddess of Death
Hela calls upon her full divine power, instantly gaining 1000 temporary HP and summoning a storm of Nightsword Thorns that rain down on a targeted area. The ult combines the defensive 1000 HP buffer with offensive AoE damage, making it simultaneously the best defensive and offensive ultimate in the Duelist roster. The temporary HP absorbs incoming damage first before her base HP is affected — allowing her to survive burst combos that would instantly eliminate any other hero. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)
Temp HP granted: 1000 Type: AoE + self-buff Unique: Dual offensive/defensive
💡 The most powerful use of Goddess of Death is reactive — activate it the moment you're being burst down rather than proactively. A Hela at 20 HP who activates her ult becomes a 1020 HP target instantly. Enemies who committed their entire burst combo to killing you are now fighting a nearly full-HP opponent. [[0]](#__0)
TEAM-UP
Asgardian Might — with Thor & Loki
When paired with Thor and/or Loki, Hela gains access to the Asgardian Might team-up ability. This synergy enhances her Nightsword Thorn damage output and grants additional effects tied to Asgardian divine power. The team-up rewards running an Asgard-themed composition and amplifies Hela's already dominant primary fire damage to even higher levels. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)
💡 Thor is Hela's best team-up partner — his Mjolnir throw stuns enemies in place, giving Hela a guaranteed two-headshot window on any stunned target. A Thor stun into Hela headshot is one of the fastest and most reliable kill confirmations in the game.

Two-Tap Headshot Loop

  • 1
    Establish high ground before the fight begins pre-fight
    Hela's primary fire is most effective from elevated positions where enemies must look upward to target her. High ground also increases the distance between her and diving heroes, giving her more time to respond with Soul Drainer or Astral Flock before they close the gap. [[0]](#__0)
  • 2
    Identify the lowest HP non-Vanguard target in the enemy backline 0.0s
    Prioritize supports and Duelists over Vanguards. Hela's two-tap is wasted on tanks — focus on the squishiest target regardless of how far back they're positioned. Her effective range reaches further than almost any other hero. [[0]](#__0) [[2]](#__2)
  • 3
    Land two consecutive headshots within 1 second LMB → LMB 0.5s apart
    The fire rate is exactly 2 rounds per second — meaning the second shot fires 0.5 seconds after the first. Two headshots deal 280 damage total, eliminating any 275 HP hero instantly. Jiggle peek corners between shots to avoid return fire during the 0.5-second window. [[0]](#__0)
  • 4
    Immediately acquire the next priority target and repeat
    After each elimination, immediately identify the next lowest HP target. Hela's value compounds with consecutive eliminations — each kill reduces the enemy team's healing output and creates more exposed targets for follow-up two-taps. [[0]](#__0)
Pro tip: The two-tap loop is Hela's entire game plan. Every other ability exists to protect this loop — Soul Drainer stops dives, Astral Flock escapes burst, and Goddess of Death extends the loop through situations that would kill any other hero. If you're not landing two headshots per second on priority targets, you're not playing Hela correctly. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)

Piercing Night Anti-Dive

  • 1
    Identify a diving melee hero approaching your position pre-engage
    Watch for Spider-Man, Black Panther, Psylocke, or any melee Duelist flanking toward you. The anti-dive combo requires you to react before they close the gap — if they reach melee range before you activate it, the combo fails. [[0]](#__0)
  • 2
    Stick all 4 Piercing Night projectiles onto the diver RMB 0.0s
    Fire Piercing Night directly at the diving hero. Their forward momentum actually helps — they're moving toward you, making it easier to land all 4 projectiles on their body. Each projectile sticks and will detonate after 3 seconds for 35 damage each (140 total explosion damage). [[0]](#__0)
  • 3
    Immediately activate Astral Flock to escape SHIFT ~0.2s
    The moment Piercing Night lands, activate Astral Flock and dash away from the diver. You are completely invincible during the dash — the diver cannot follow you effectively, and the 3-second explosion timer is already counting down on their body. [[0]](#__0)
  • 4
    Resume primary fire as explosions detonate LMB ~3s after RMB
    After transforming back from Astral Flock, immediately resume primary fire on the diver. The 140 explosion damage has already chipped them significantly — a single headshot follow-up eliminates most 250 HP Duelists who attempted the dive. [[0]](#__0) [[2]](#__2)
Why it works: Piercing Night + Astral Flock converts a dangerous dive situation into a free elimination. The diver commits their mobility cooldowns to reach Hela, only to find her invincible and escaping — then takes 140 explosion damage while she resumes shooting from a new position. Most dive heroes cannot survive this counter-sequence. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)

Soul Drainer Stun Combo

  • 1
    Identify an advancing Vanguard or grouped cluster pre-engage
    Soul Drainer's 3-meter AoE stun is most valuable against Vanguards pushing your position or grouped enemies standing close together. The 0.3-second stun window is short — only use it when you have a clear headshot angle ready to follow up immediately. [[0]](#__0)
  • 2
    Land Soul Drainer directly on the target E 0.0s
    Arc the Hel sphere directly onto the target's position. The 40m/s projectile speed requires slight lead time at range — aim slightly ahead of moving targets. The 40 AoE damage plus stun creates a guaranteed headshot window on any stunned enemy. [[0]](#__0)
  • 3
    Immediately fire two headshots during the 0.3s stun window LMB → LMB 0.0–0.3s
    The 0.3-second stun is exactly enough time for one guaranteed headshot. The second shot fires 0.5 seconds after the first — meaning the stun expires before the second shot, but the first headshot is guaranteed. Soul Drainer + one headshot deals 40 + 140 = 180 damage, eliminating most 175 HP supports outright. [[0]](#__0) [[2]](#__2)
  • 4
    Follow up with Piercing Night if the target survives RMB
    If the target survives the stun combo, immediately fire Piercing Night to stick 4 projectiles onto them. The 140 delayed explosion damage combined with the initial stun combo damage eliminates virtually any non-Vanguard hero in the game. [[0]](#__0)
Why it works: Soul Drainer's stun converts Hela's aim-dependent headshot into a guaranteed hit. Against enemies who are actively dodging her primary fire, the stun forces a stationary target for the critical 0.3-second window. The 40 AoE damage also softens the target, reducing the number of headshots needed to secure the elimination. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)

Reactive Goddess of Death Expert

Using the 1000 temporary HP ult as a reactive survival tool rather than an offensive ultimate — the highest-skill Hela technique.

  • 1
    Monitor your HP constantly throughout the fight ongoing
    Reactive Goddess of Death requires constant HP awareness. You need to activate the ult at the exact moment you're being burst down — too early wastes the HP buffer on non-critical damage, too late and you're already dead before it activates. [[0]](#__0)
  • 2
    Identify when enemies commit their burst combo to eliminating you
    Learn to recognize burst patterns — a Spider-Man web combo, a Punisher ult, a coordinated focus fire. The moment you identify that enemies are committing to eliminating you in the next 1–2 seconds, that is the activation window. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)
  • 3
    Activate Goddess of Death at 30–50 HP Q 30–50 HP threshold
    Activate the ult when your HP drops to 30–50. The 1000 temporary HP activates instantly — you go from near-death to 1030–1050 effective HP in a single frame. Enemies who committed their entire burst combo to killing you are now fighting a nearly full-HP opponent with zero cooldowns remaining.
  • 4
    Immediately resume two-tap headshot loop during the 1000 HP window LMB → LMB
    After activating, don't panic — you now have 1000 temporary HP absorbing all incoming damage. Use this window aggressively: the enemies who just tried to kill you have spent their cooldowns and are now vulnerable. Two-tap the lowest HP attacker immediately while they're exposed.
Why it works: Reactive Goddess of Death is the single highest-skill technique in Hela's kit because it requires reading enemy intent and reacting in under a second. When executed correctly, it completely invalidates the enemy team's burst combo — they spent every cooldown to kill a 250 HP target and instead created a 1250 HP opponent who is now shooting back at full effectiveness.
Common Questions

FAQ

Hela is not recommended for beginners. Her entire power budget is concentrated in headshot damage — body-shot Hela deals less effective damage than most other Duelists and contributes almost nothing to her team. She requires consistent aim, map knowledge for high-ground positioning, and the game sense to use Astral Flock and Goddess of Death reactively rather than proactively. Players who invest time in her mechanics are rewarded with the highest individual carry potential of any Duelist in the game.

Shields are Hela's hardest counter — Doctor Strange, Magneto, and Groot shields completely negate her primary fire. The solution is off-angles: find elevated or flanking positions where you can shoot around the shield rather than into it. If the enemy team is running a full shield composition and you cannot find off-angles, consider switching to a hero who deals with shields better. Hela should never be force-picked into a shield-heavy composition.
