Moon Knight
Complete Guide

Marc Spector — the avatar of Khonshu who bounces crescent darts between grouped enemies, plants sacred Ankhs to amplify every projectile, and calls down the Hand of Khonshu to haunt entire teams with his ultimate.

If you want a Duelist who charges his ultimate faster than anyone else, punishes grouped teams harder than any other mid-range hero, and can outright ignore enemy defensive ultimates with his own — Moon Knight is the apex predator of grouped compositions.

His skill expression is almost entirely about Ankh placement and target grouping: a Moon Knight who consistently places his Ankh out of enemy line of sight while maintaining bounce angles on grouped enemies will top damage charts every game. His ultimate is uniquely powerful because it deals damage faster than most healing ultimates can compensate. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)

Kit Breakdown

Abilities & Skills

PASSIVE
Night Glider
Moon Knight can glide through the air by holding the jump button after a leap, extending his airtime significantly. Combined with his double jump (Rising Leap) and Moonlight Hook grapple, this passive gives him three independent vertical movement tools — making him one of the most mobile Duelists for reaching and maintaining high ground positions. [[0]](#__0)
💡 Use the glide to extend your aerial hang time after a hookshot, letting you survey the battlefield and choose your landing spot. The combination of hookshot → double jump → glide can reach almost any elevated position on any map in the game.
LMB
Crescent Dart
Moon Knight fires three crescent darts simultaneously in a straight trajectory. Each dart deals 25 damage and can bounce between enemies and Ankhs within 7 meters, with up to 3 bounces per dart. Each bounce deals 20% less damage than the previous hit. The darts can critically hit, and every hit on an enemy generates ultimate charge — making Vanguards the ideal primary target for farming ult meter rapidly. [[0]](#__0)
Damage: 25 per dart Darts per shot: 3 Speed: 150m/s Ammo: 30 Max bounces: 3 Bounce falloff: -20% per bounce
💡 Always target Vanguards with primary fire — their large bodies are easy to hit consistently, and every hit generates ult charge. Moon Knight should be the first player to have his ultimate in almost every fight when played correctly. Never try to snipe mobile targets at long range; stay in the 10–30m sweet spot. [[0]](#__0)
RMB
Moon Blade
Moon Knight launches a single Moon Blade projectile that deals 80 damage on hit and bounces between enemies and Ankhs within 7 meters (up to 3 bounces, -20% damage per bounce). Each hit also grants 25 bonus HP (up to 100 max), which decays at 20 HP/s after 3 seconds. This is his highest single-hit damage ability and his primary self-sustain tool. The bonus HP mechanic rewards aggressive play — landing Moon Blade hits consistently keeps him alive without supports. [[0]](#__0)
Damage: 80 Bonus HP per hit: 25 Max bonus HP: 100 HP decay: 20/s after 3s Cooldown: 6s Max bounces: 3
💡 Moon Blade is your execute tool AND your sustain tool simultaneously. Use it on grouped enemies near your Ankh to bounce-chain the 80 damage across multiple targets while stacking bonus HP from each hit. Against a single isolated target, save it for the kill shot below 80 HP. [[0]](#__0)
E
Ancient Ankh
Moon Knight places a sacred Ankh on any surface. The Ankh acts as a bounce node — all of Moon Knight's projectiles (Crescent Dart and Moon Blade) bounce off it and toward the nearest enemy, amplifying his damage output dramatically against grouped targets. The Ankh has its own HP pool and can be destroyed by enemies. When destroyed, it goes on a 12-second cooldown — the longest downtime of any ability in his kit. The Ankh is the single most important ability in his entire kit. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)
Type: Bounce node Cooldown: 12s (on destroy) Placement: Any surface
💡 Place the Ankh out of enemy line of sight but within bounce range of where enemies are standing — on a wall behind a corner, above a doorframe, or on a ceiling. If enemies can see the Ankh, they will destroy it immediately. An Ankh they can't see is an Ankh that keeps firing. [[0]](#__0)
SHIFT
Moonlight Hook
Moon Knight fires a grappling hook that pulls him toward the target surface or enemy at high speed. This is his primary tool for reaching elevated positions, escaping dives, and repositioning mid-fight. Unlike most grapple abilities, Moonlight Hook can target both surfaces and enemies — hitting an enemy directly with the hook deals damage and closes the gap instantly for a follow-up burst. [[0]](#__0)
Type: Grapple Target: Surface or enemy Cooldown: ~8s
💡 Use Moonlight Hook to reach wall and ceiling positions for Ankh placement that ground-level enemies can't easily reach or destroy. The best Ankh spots are always accessible only by hookshot — if you can walk to the Ankh placement, so can the enemy.
Q — ULT
Hand of Khonshu
Moon Knight calls upon Khonshu to rain crescent projectiles down on a targeted area, dealing rapid damage to all enemies caught in the zone. After the Season 1 buffs, Hand of Khonshu deals damage so quickly that it out-damages Luna Snow's healing ultimate — meaning enemies cannot survive inside it even with defensive ults active. This makes it one of the only ultimates in the game that kills through enemy defensive abilities. The ult charges faster than any other Duelist's due to Crescent Dart's high bounce-hit ult generation. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)
Type: AoE zone Damage speed: Beats healing ults Charge rate: Fastest Duelist
💡 Hand of Khonshu is most powerful when combined with a teammate stun or root to prevent enemies from walking out of the zone. Without CC, aware enemies can simply move out of the AoE. With CC, it's an unavoidable team wipe that even Luna Snow's ult cannot prevent. [[0]](#__0)
TEAM-UP
Full Moon — with Scarlet Witch
When paired with Scarlet Witch, Moon Knight gains access to the Full Moon team-up ability — enhancing his kit with additional chaos energy effects that amplify his projectile damage and Ankh interactions. This team-up synergizes with Moon Knight's bounce-chain playstyle by increasing the damage multiplier on each successive bounce hit. [[1]](#__1)
💡 In a Moon Knight + Scarlet Witch composition, coordinate Scarlet Witch's Reality Erasure with Moon Knight's Hand of Khonshu — one ultimate forces enemies to scatter, the other punishes them for grouping. Together they cover both scenarios.

Counters & Matchups

✅ Moon Knight Counters
🛡️
Grouped Vanguards
Large hitboxes make Crescent Dart headshots trivial to land, charging ult faster than any other matchup. Bouncing darts off a Vanguard into nearby supports is his highest-value primary fire scenario. [[0]](#__0)
❄️
Luna Snow (Defensive Ult)
Hand of Khonshu's post-buff damage rate outpaces Luna Snow's healing ult — enemies inside her defensive ult still die to Moon Knight's ultimate. [[0]](#__0)
🦑
Namor's Monstro Spawn
Bouncing Moon Blade destroys both Namor turrets simultaneously with minimal effort — Moon Knight is Namor's hardest counter in the entire roster. [[1]](#__1)
👥
Objective-Stacking Teams
Teams that group tightly on objectives are punished hardest by Crescent Dart bounce chains — each dart can hit 3+ enemies per shot when they're within 7 meters of each other. [[0]](#__0)
❌ Hard Counters Moon Knight
🌪️
Storm / Iron Man (Aerial)
Ankh must be placed on surfaces — it provides zero bounce value against aerial heroes. Both primary and secondary fire are difficult to land on fast-moving fliers at range. [[0]](#__0)
🥷
Psylocke
Close-range burst combo kills Moon Knight before he can set up his Ankh. Her flanking playstyle directly counters his need for time to reach and establish high ground positions. [[1]](#__1)
📏
Spread-Out Teams
Enemies who play spread out deny all bounce chain value. If no two enemies are within 7 meters of each other, Crescent Dart becomes a single-target low-damage projectile. [[0]](#__0)
👁️
Ankh-Aware Players
Any player who immediately destroys the Ankh on sight puts Moon Knight on a 12-second cooldown where he is barely a threat. Ankh awareness is the single most effective counter to his entire kit. [[0]](#__0)

Ankh Setup Loop

  • 1
    Reach high ground with Moonlight Hook before the fight SHIFT pre-fight
    Always establish high ground before placing your Ankh. Elevated positions give you bounce angles that ground-level enemies can't easily block, and make the Ankh harder to spot and destroy. [[0]](#__0)
  • 2
    Place Ancient Ankh out of enemy line of sight E pre-fight
    Stick the Ankh on a wall, ceiling, or surface that enemies cannot see from their natural sightlines. The Ankh only needs to be within 7 meters of where enemies are standing — it does not need to be visible to them to bounce your projectiles. [[0]](#__0)
  • 3
    Spam Crescent Dart at grouped enemies and Vanguards LMB ongoing
    Fire primary fire continuously at the nearest Vanguard. Every hit generates ult charge. Every dart that bounces off the Ankh hits additional targets. Focus on headshots — they trigger no additional cooldown but deal double damage and generate extra ult charge. [[0]](#__0)
  • 4
    Immediately redeploy Ankh to a new position when destroyed E
    When enemies destroy your Ankh, never place the replacement in the same spot. Move to a new surface at a different angle — enemies who just destroyed the first Ankh will look in the same location for the second one. [[0]](#__0)
Pro tip: The Ankh setup loop is the foundation of every Moon Knight game. Your entire damage output depends on the Ankh surviving long enough to bounce projectiles. An Ankh that lasts 10 seconds generates more value than three Ankhs that each last 2 seconds in obvious positions. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)

Bounce Chain Burst

  • 1
    Confirm Ankh is active and 2+ enemies are within 7m of each other pre-burst
    The bounce chain only generates multi-target value when enemies are close together. Wait for the right moment — a grouped team on an objective is the ideal trigger. [[0]](#__0)
  • 2
    Fire Moon Blade at the primary target RMB 0.0s
    Lead with Moon Blade — 80 damage on the primary hit, then bounces to nearby enemies and the Ankh for additional hits. Each hit grants 25 bonus HP, so a full bounce chain can give you up to 100 bonus HP instantly. [[0]](#__0)
  • 3
    Follow immediately with Crescent Dart burst LMB ~0.3s
    After Moon Blade, dump a full Crescent Dart burst at the same target cluster. The darts bounce off the Ankh and between enemies — with 3 darts per shot and up to 3 bounces each, a single shot can hit the same group of 3 enemies for 9 total dart impacts. [[0]](#__0)
  • 4
    Finish lowest HP target with headshot primary fire LMB
    After the burst, identify the lowest HP target and aim for their head. Moon Knight's primary fire headshots deal double damage — at close range against a chipped target, a single headshot burst is often enough to secure the elimination. [[0]](#__0)
Why it works: Moon Blade + Crescent Dart burst is Moon Knight's highest single-rotation damage output. The Moon Blade bounce chains generate bonus HP while dealing damage, and the immediate Crescent Dart follow-up catches enemies before they can react to the first hit. Against a grouped team near an Ankh, this rotation deals more effective damage than almost any other Duelist's opener. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)

Backline Assassination

  • 1
    Identify an unaware support target in the enemy backline pre-engage
    Moon Knight's assassination combo only works against targets who are not actively dodging. Look for supports who are focused on healing, not watching their flanks. An aware target can dodge his slow projectiles consistently. [[0]](#__0)
  • 2
    Reach the backline via Moonlight Hook to an elevated angle SHIFT
    Don't approach at ground level — hookshot to an elevated position above or behind the enemy support. Attacking from above breaks their natural sightline and gives your darts a downward angle that's harder to dodge. [[0]](#__0)
  • 3
    Place Ankh on the nearest surface before engaging E ~0.5s
    Even in the backline, place your Ankh on the nearest wall or ceiling before firing. A backline Ankh that bounces darts between the support and a nearby Vanguard doubles your effective damage output in the assassination window. [[0]](#__0)
  • 4
    Moon Blade → Crescent Dart headshots → escape with hookshot RMB → LMB → SHIFT
    Execute the burst combo: Moon Blade for the initial 80 damage hit, then Crescent Dart headshots to finish. If the target survives, use Moonlight Hook immediately to escape before their team can respond. Never stay in the backline after the burst — retreat and reset. [[0]](#__0)
Why it works: Moon Knight's triple mobility tools (hookshot, double jump, glide) make him one of the hardest Duelists to catch after a backline assassination attempt. The key is the unaware target requirement — his slow projectiles mean aware enemies can dodge, but unaware supports caught in the burst have no time to react before the combo lands. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)

CC-Chained Hand of Khonshu Expert

Turning Hand of Khonshu from a good ultimate into an unavoidable team wipe through coordinated crowd control.

  • 1
    Farm ult charge by bouncing darts off Vanguards all game LMB ongoing
    Moon Knight should reach his ultimate first in almost every fight. Prioritize Vanguard targets with primary fire throughout the entire game — their large hitboxes and slow movement make them the most reliable ult charge source. [[0]](#__0)
  • 2
    Coordinate Hand of Khonshu with teammate CC timing
    Communicate with your Groot or Doctor Strange before the fight. The plan: they CC first on a grouped cluster, you activate Hand of Khonshu immediately after. Without CC, aware enemies simply walk out of the AoE zone. [[0]](#__0)
  • 3
    Activate Hand of Khonshu the moment CC lands Q 0.0s after CC
    Zero delay between CC landing and ult activation. Every second of delay is a second enemies spend trying to escape the zone. The ult's damage rate is fast enough to kill through Luna Snow's healing ult — no defensive ability can save enemies who are locked in place. [[0]](#__0)
  • 4
    Follow up on survivors with Moon Blade + Crescent Dart burst RMB + LMB
    Any enemies who survived the ult are now critically low HP and likely disoriented. Immediately execute the bounce chain burst on the lowest HP survivor — your ult charge is already regenerating from the ult hits, so your next Hand of Khonshu will arrive faster than any other Duelist's. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)
Why it works: Hand of Khonshu's post-Season 1 buff damage rate is uniquely powerful — it is one of the only ultimates in Marvel Rivals that kills enemies even through defensive healing ultimates like Luna Snow's. Combined with CC that prevents escape, it is a guaranteed team wipe on any grouped composition. Moon Knight's fast ult charge rate means this combo can happen multiple times per game. FAQ

Moon Knight has a moderate learning curve. His primary fire is straightforward, but his effectiveness is almost entirely dependent on Ankh placement — a skill that requires map knowledge and game sense to execute well. Beginners can still deal decent damage with basic Ankh placement, but his full potential requires understanding bounce angles, hidden placement spots, and when to reposition. His 52.2% win rate and 8.53 KDA suggest he rewards players who invest time in learning his mechanics.
