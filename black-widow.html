Black Widow
Complete Guide

Natasha Romanova — the only sniper in Marvel Rivals with a full melee kit. Armed with the Red Room Rifle and her electric batons, Black Widow demands elite mechanical skill and constant repositioning to reach her ceiling.

If you want the highest mechanical expression of any sniper — a hero who can one-shot supports with a headshot when damage-boosted, then finish stragglers with a melee combo — Black Widow rewards dedicated players who master her positioning game. Just know: she's currently D-Tier without a Mantis pocket. [[0]](#__0) [[2]](#__2)

Black Widow has 250 HP — the same as most supports — making her extremely fragile. Her Red Room Rifle deals 120 damage per shot with zero falloff when scoped, meaning she's equally lethal at any range. The critical limitation: 120 damage doesn't one-shot any hero. Kit Breakdown

Abilities & Skills

LMB (Baton)
Widow's Bite Baton
DMG: 45/strike
Rate: 2 strikes/sec
Range: 3m melee
Special: Pierces shields
Black Widow's electric baton melee attack dealing 45 damage per strike at 2 strikes per second. The critical special property: baton strikes deal damage through shields — meaning heroes like Dr. Strange cannot block them with their shield ability. This makes the baton a surprisingly effective tool against shielding tanks who jump on you. [[0]](#__0)
💡 Swap to batons in three situations: (1) a shielding tank jumps on you — batons bypass their shield; (2) you're in close range and out of rifle ammo; (3) you're dueling a fast melee hero whose movement is hard to track with the rifle. [[0]](#__0)
LMB (Rifle)
Red Room Rifle
DMG: 120/shot
Rate: 0.83 shots/sec
Mag: 15 rounds
ADS time: 0.3s
Falloff: None (scoped)
Crit: Yes
Black Widow's primary weapon — a sniper rifle dealing 120 damage per shot with zero falloff when scoped. This is the only hitscan weapon in Marvel Rivals with no damage falloff, giving her a unique advantage at extreme ranges. Unscoped shots have falloff starting at 10m (down to 50% at 20m), so always scope for long-range engagements. The slow 0.83 shots/sec fire rate means every shot must count. [[0]](#__0)
💡 At close range (under 10m), fire unscoped — falloff hasn't kicked in yet and you skip the 0.3s ADS time. Use scoped shots exclusively for medium-to-long range. Look for low-HP enemies — even a body shot at 120 damage finishes most supports below 130 HP. [[0]](#__0)
⚡ Damage Boost Math: Without boost: 120 body / ~180 headshot — cannot one-shot any hero. With Mantis boost: headshots reach ~250+ damage — one-shots all 250 HP heroes. With Rocket Raccoon ult boost: can one-shot 300 HP heroes, but only for the ult window duration. Only 15 heroes total can be one-shot by Widow even with a standard damage boost. [[0]](#__0)
SHIFT
Edge Dancer (Zip Kick)
Type: Gap closer + stun
Effect: Brief stun on target
Follow-up: Rifle shot window
Black Widow zips to a target and delivers a kick that briefly stuns them. This is her primary mobility and melee combo initiator. The stun window after the kick is the key mechanic — the enemy cannot dodge during it, creating a guaranteed follow-up shot. This is also her escape tool when divers close the gap. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)
💡 Always follow Edge Dancer's zip kick with an immediate rifle shot — the stun prevents the enemy from dodging, making it a near-guaranteed hit. This is the core of her kill combo: zip in → kick (stun) → headshot. [[0]](#__0)
E
Electro-Web / Grapple
Type: Utility / Mobility
Use: Repositioning + escape
Black Widow fires her grappling hook to quickly reposition or escape dangerous situations. This is her primary survival tool — when a diver closes the gap, grapple to high ground or cover immediately. Constant repositioning is the core of Black Widow's playstyle; staying in one spot is a death sentence. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)
💡 Never stay in the same position for more than 2–3 shots. Black Widow's greatest defensive tool is unpredictability — the enemy team should never know where her next shot is coming from. Grapple after every kill to a new angle. [[0]](#__0)
Q — ULT
Widow's Hour (Sniper Ult)
Effect: Slows enemies
Fire: Slow-moving projectile
Rating: Nearly useless (community consensus)
Black Widow charges up and fires a slow-moving projectile that slows enemies in an area. The community consensus and top Widow players agree: this ult is nearly useless in its current state. The charge-up animation telegraphs it clearly, the projectile is slow enough for enemies to dodge, and the camera switches to first-person awkwardly. Most top Widow players use it only as a follow-up to finish enemies that a headshot didn't kill. [[0]](#__0) [[3]](#__3)
⚠️ Best Ult Usage: The only reliable use for Widow's ult is to combo its slow with a teammate's AoE ult — e.g., slow a grouped cluster then have Namor drop his ult on them. Otherwise, use it purely as a finisher on low-HP targets that escaped your headshot. Do not use it as an opener. [[0]](#__0)

Combo Guide

Long-Range Snipe Loop

  • 1
    Confirm Mantis damage boost is active Pre-engage
    Before engaging, confirm your Mantis has damage boost on you. Without it, your headshots won't one-shot 250 HP heroes and you're just a slow sniper with no kill pressure. [[0]](#__0)
  • 2
    Find a long sightline from high ground 0.0s
    Position on high ground with a clear sightline to the enemy backline. Black Widow has zero falloff when scoped — the longer the range, the safer she is from divers. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)
  • 3
    ADS and headshot priority target RMB → LMB ~0.3s ADS
    Scope in (0.3s ADS time) and aim for the head of a 250 HP support. With Mantis boost, this is a one-shot kill. Prioritize isolated supports who aren't behind cover. [[0]](#__0)
  • 4
    Immediately grapple to a new angle E ~0.5s after shot
    The moment you fire, grapple to a new position. Don't wait to see if you got the kill — the enemy team is already looking for your location. Unpredictability is your defense. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)
  • 5
    Repeat from new angle — never the same spot twice Loop
    Establish a rotation of 3–4 positions and cycle through them. Ping enemy locations to your team as you spot them from your elevated angles. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)
Positioning principle: Black Widow's only defense is not being where the enemy expects her to be. Every shot reveals your position — grapple immediately after firing. The players who die on Widow are the ones who stay still admiring their shot. [[0]](#__0) [[1]](#__1)

Edge Dancer → Headshot Kill

Widow's most reliable kill combo — the stun from Edge Dancer creates a guaranteed headshot window. [[0]](#__0)

  • 1
    Identify an isolated low-HP target 0.0s
    Look for a support or DPS below 150 HP separated from their team. This combo works at any HP, but is most reliable as a finisher. [[0]](#__0)
  • 2
    Edge Dancer zip kick SHIFT ~0.2s
    Zip to the target and deliver the kick. The brief stun activates immediately on landing — the enemy cannot move or dodge during this window. [[0]](#__0)
  • 3
    Immediate headshot during stun window LMB (Rifle) ~0.3s
    Fire your rifle shot the instant the kick lands. The stun prevents dodging — this is a near-guaranteed headshot. With Mantis boost, this kills any 250 HP hero instantly. [[0]](#__0)
  • 4
    Follow up with baton strikes if not dead LMB (Baton) ~0.5s
    If the headshot didn't kill (e.g. target had more HP or no boost), immediately swap to batons and finish with 2–3 strikes at 45 damage each. [[0]](#__0)
  • 5
    Grapple out immediately E ~1.0s
    The moment the target is dead (or if you missed), grapple to safety. You're now in melee range of the enemy team — staying is suicide. [[0]](#__0)
Why the stun matters: Without Edge Dancer's stun, landing a headshot on a moving target at close range is extremely difficult. The stun turns a hard mechanical challenge into a straightforward point-blank shot. Always follow Edge Dancer with an immediate rifle shot — never waste the stun window. [[0]](#__0)

Ult → Namor AoE Combo Expert

The only high-value use of Widow's ult — requires pre-game coordination with your Namor or Storm. [[0]](#__0)

  • 1
    Coordinate with Namor/Storm before the fight Pre-teamfight
    Tell your Namor or Storm to hold their ult. Widow's ult slow is only valuable as a setup — without a follow-up AoE, it's wasted. [[0]](#__0)
  • 2
    Wait for 3+ enemies to group on objective Timing
    Widow's ult slow is most valuable when it hits multiple enemies. Wait for a natural cluster — don't force it on a single target. [[0]](#__0)
  • 3
    Fire Widow's Hour on the cluster Q ~0.5s charge
    Charge up and fire the slow projectile at the grouped enemies. Call out "ult fired" to your Namor immediately. [[0]](#__0)
  • 4
    Namor drops AoE ult on slowed cluster Immediate follow-up
    Slowed enemies cannot escape Namor's AoE ult. This combo can wipe an entire team in seconds — the slow removes their only counter-play (movement). [[0]](#__0)
The only good Widow ult: Used alone, Widow's ult is nearly useless — the slow projectile is dodgeable and the camera switch is disorienting. Used as a setup for Namor or Storm's AoE ult, it becomes a teamfight-winning play. This coordination is what separates good Widow players from great ones. [[0]](#__0) [[3]](#__3)
